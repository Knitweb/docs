## Executive Summary

### The vision

**Markets of Truth** is a serverless, peer-to-peer, agentic *truth market* — a content-addressed game in which humans and autonomous LLM traders stake **PLS** to (a) curate and verify **CLAIMS** and (b) forecast **OUTCOMES**, and in which a claim resolves not because an operator says so but because its resolution is *re-executed* by a Byzantine committee, survives a dispute window, and is scored by a calibrated reliability oracle. It is the agent-economy flagship knitweb on the Knitweb Pulse substrate — the sibling to the chemistry game *molgang*. Where molgang knits chemistry concepts into a verified graph, Markets of Truth knits **claims and their resolutions** into one. The slogan is the same load-bearing one the substrate earns everywhere: **the game *is* the protocol.** Proposing a claim is a real `FabricNode.weave`; staking a position is a dual-signed `Knit`; a market's lifecycle is an immutable `Fiber`; resolution is a `pouw.quorum` BFT verdict over a re-executed outcome; a dispute is a real `DisputeWindowLedger` window with slashable collateral; and a reward accrues to *verified-useful curation* through `Treasury.reward_verified_work` — never to a wager. Markets of Truth is not a betting venue wrapped around a blockchain; it is an L5 domain plugin (a *knitweb*) that imports one engine and re-implements nothing.

The ambition of this plan is to take Markets of Truth from a substrate that already settles, gossips, verifies and mints — to a genuinely **top-10-calibre dapp** in the emerging "information-finance" (InfoFi) category, distinguished by three properties almost no shipping prediction product combines:

1. **No exchange server.** There is no house, no order-matching backend, no operator who can front-run a resolution, censor a market, halt withdrawals, or be subpoenaed into seizing the venue. Claims, stakes, positions and resolutions live as canonical Knit/Web records replicated over the Pulse gossip mesh (Erlay reconcile, gossipsub, anti-entropy backstop), reaching into the browser and surviving NAT. The defensibility of every honesty claim in this plan rests on this single structural fact.
2. **Oracle soundness as a first-class primitive.** The category's graveyard is full of oracle failures — a single 2025 UMA dispute saw one holder cast ~25% of votes across three accounts to force a $7M payout on a demonstrably false resolution; a January 2026 cross-venue spot-pump netted a $233k Polymarket profit. Markets of Truth refuses a trusted oracle entirely: outcomes are settled by **PoUW re-execution + dispute window + LENS reliability scoring + a personhood anti-sybil gate**, not by a token-weighted vote a whale can dominate.
3. **Agentic LLM traders as first-class peers.** By mid-2026, machines — not humans — are the defining users of open prediction markets (AI agents account for 30%+ of Polymarket wallet activity; 14 of the 20 most-profitable wallets are bots). Markets of Truth is built for that world from the floor up: an agentic trader signs the same secp256k1 key, settles the same PLS Knit, passes the same personhood/agenthood gate, and is held to the same verification quorum and dispute window as a human curator.

### The thesis

A truth market is only as trustworthy as its weakest settlement seam. It can have beautiful markets, lively agents and deep liquidity, yet if a single operator can quietly rewrite an outcome, front-run a resolution, or inflate a reputation, the whole edifice is theatre. The structural claim beneath this product is therefore: **a credible information market reduces to four irreducible engineering requirements — settlement no operator can rewrite, resolution no operator can adjudicate, identity no one can flood, and reasoning anyone can re-check — and Knitweb Pulse already expresses each one as a native primitive rather than as a service to be trusted.** The integer-only Braid/Knit/Fiber ledger is settlement; PoUW re-execution + the `DisputeWindowLedger` is resolution; the personhood gate is anti-sybil identity; the LENS reliability lobe is re-checkable reasoning; and **PLS**, minted *only* as a demand-gated, now epoch-bound reward for verified-useful curation, is the activity-accounting unit that ties verified work to value without ever becoming a chip. Markets of Truth is the proof that this substrate carries a regulatory-sensitive consumer product honestly — the showcase for an agent economy that pays for *truth-seeking work*, not for luck.

### What is built vs. what is to-build

We are deliberately honest about maturity. The substrate's *spine* is real and well-tested; its *body* — the market engine, the matching path, the oracle reliability layer, the agent SDK, the browser transport — is the work this plan funds.

| Already production-grade (ACTIVE) | Adapter-only / scaffold | Stubbed / to-build |
|---|---|---|
| `core` (canonical/crypto/pulse, now epoch-bound R1), full integer ledger (Braid/Knit/Fiber), p2p base/transport/wire/reconcile/mesh/kademlia/discovery, fabric Web + FabricNode, all PoUW verify/quorum/committee/sampling/challenge/**dispute**/collateral/escrow, token mint (epoch-bound Treasury), **VBANK** (poll/ranked/liquid/tally), crowdfunding, **personhood** gate | `personhood.verifier` (VC/ZK backend injected), `pouw.scheduler`/`marketplace` (tested, not in a live epoch loop), `interpret`/**LENS** (deterministic retrieve + gated distill + feedback; calibrated-confidence + abstention layer in design = lens #5–8, float-quarantined), synaptic/anchor/gateway/sdk (thin), `knitweb_monitor` (liveness viewer) | the `knitwebs/markets` claim/market/resolution plugin (sibling of the `chemistry` stub), the deterministic matching/AMM engine, the LENS reliability *oracle* (calibrated confidence + abstention + provenance-cited scoring), the agentic-trader SDK, MONITOR-as-integrity-instrument, the browser-P2P transport |

The single most consequential dependency is no longer the monetary seam — **R1 (epoch-bound issuance, PR #196) has landed**, binding `Treasury.reward_verified_work` to a per-epoch `epoch_mint_cap` on the Beat, so the activity→money loop the whole stack exists to express is now closed. The load-bearing open work for *this* product is oracle soundness: turning the deterministic LENS retrieve/distill pipeline into a **calibrated, abstaining, provenance-cited reliability oracle** (lens #5–8), and wiring the deterministic resolution path so that any peer can reproduce any outcome. That is buildable as new packages under the existing `pouw.dispute`, `interpret` and `pouw.quorum` seams, none of which needs a protocol change.

### The regulatory line

A truth/prediction market is regulatory-sensitive by construction — it brushes against gambling law, the CFTC's event-contract regime, and securities law. This plan draws the line in plain terms and designs to it, rather than hoping to be overlooked. **Markets of Truth is a decentralized information-curation and skill-based verified-work venue with an activity-accounting unit — not a gambling product and not an event-contract securities exchange.** Three design facts carry that claim: (1) PLS is minted *only* as a bounded, demand-gated reward for committee-verified useful curation — there is no premine, no admin mint, no path to buy chips, and no operator booking the other side of a wager; (2) reward accrues to *verified-useful curation and calibrated forecasting skill* re-checkable by any peer, with resolution settled by re-execution rather than by a house; and (3) there is no venue operator at all — no central exchange to license, halt, or be made the counterparty. PLS is an activity-accounting unit throughout, never a speculative instrument, and Chapter 13 addresses jurisdiction and compliance honestly rather than rhetorically.

### The ask

This plan requests resourcing for a four-phase programme (**P0 Foundation → P1 Serverless Market MVP → P2 Agentic + Economy → P3 Scale & Top-10**) whose guiding principle is **continuity, not rewrite.** Concretely, the funded work is: (1) build the ~14 new packages catalogued in the master table below — the `markets` knitweb, the deterministic matching/resolution engine, the LENS reliability oracle, the agentic-trader SDK, MONITOR-as-integrity-instrument, the browser-P2P transport and the supporting glue — each as its own versioned package with its own test suite; (2) ship a serverless market MVP, then the agentic economy, then a measured top-10 push; (3) keep the honest framing intact on every surface — PLS as activity-accounting, oracle-soundness and anti-manipulation as first-class, the regulatory line designed-in. The remainder of this document substantiates the market case, the substrate fit, the role of each component (PULSE, LENS, MONITOR, VBANK), the resolution-and-anti-manipulation design, the gap analysis, the tokenomics-and-regulatory line, the implementation plan, and the milestone-by-milestone path to a top-10 dapp.

## 2. Market & landscape: information finance and the path to a top-10 dapp

This chapter situates **Markets of Truth** — and the Pulse substrate beneath it — inside the 2025–2026 prediction-and-information-market landscape. The thesis is not that another betting venue is needed; it is that the market has split into two halves, a *regulated-betting* half racing toward the CFTC and the sportsbooks, and an *information-curation* half (the emerging "InfoFi" thesis) that needs an oracle-grade, manipulation-resistant, agent-native substrate it does not yet have. Markets of Truth is built for the second half. We (a) define what "top-10" measures, (b) read where attention and money sit, (c) examine the three structural movements the product rides — agentic-trading, decentralized oracles, and the serverless/fully-on-chain trend — and (d) place the product against named competitors with real 2026 numbers. Throughout we are honest that this is a *regulatory-sensitive* category and that our defensibility rests on being verified-useful-work info-curation, not gambling.

### 2.1 The market exploded — and bifurcated

The numbers are no longer speculative. Polymarket and Kalshi's combined monthly volume more than quadrupled in eight months, from under **$5B in September 2025 to ~$24B by April 2026** ([TRM Labs](https://www.trmlabs.com/resources/blog/how-prediction-markets-scaled-to-usd-21b-in-monthly-volume-in-2026)). Polymarket crossed **$10.57B in a single month (March 2026)**, set a **$425M single-day record (28 Feb 2026)**, and grew monthly unique wallets to **~840,000** ([Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/polymarket/), [Token Terminal](https://tokenterminal.com/explorer/projects/polymarket/metrics/trading-volume)). Kalshi reached **~$4.5B/month** in late 2025 and **$52B in cumulative event contracts** by March 2026, raising at a **$22B valuation** ([Wikipedia: Kalshi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalshi), [insights4vc](https://insights4vc.substack.com/p/prediction-markets-at-scale-2026)). Liquidity across major platforms now exceeds **$50B annually** ([FinancialContent](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/predictstreet-2026-2-1-the-infofi-revolution-how-prediction-markets-became-the-worlds-fastest-financial-data-feed)).

But the *composition* of that volume reveals the strategic opening. On Kalshi, **sports made up ~80% of volume since July 2024 and ~90% of activity / 89% of revenue in 2025**; on Polymarket sports exceeded **60% of open interest** by October 2025 ([Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/money/Kalshi-Inc), [Gambling Insider](https://www.gamblinginsider.com/in-depth/110180/prediction-market-statistics)). The incumbents have, in practice, become **regulated sportsbooks wearing event-contract clothing** — a posture now under direct legal attack (§2.5). The genuinely *informational* use — markets that produce a verifiable public signal about elections, science, governance, or claim-truth — is the minority of incumbent volume and the majority of the **InfoFi** narrative's intellectual energy. That gap is the niche Markets of Truth occupies.

### 2.2 The "InfoFi" turn — from betting parlor to truth machine

The defining narrative of 2025–2026 is **InfoFi (Information Finance)**: the framing of information and attention as a tradeable primitive, and of prediction markets as "an oracle about what people with skin in the game actually believe" ([crypto.com research](https://crypto.com/us/research/infofi-jun-2025)). By early 2026 commentators describe prediction markets as having transitioned from "betting parlors" to a "source of truth for the global financial machine," with the InfoFi category spanning prediction markets, reputation markets, attention markets, and data-analytics ([FinancialContent: Truth Machine](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/predictstreet-2026-2-2-the-rise-of-infofi-how-prediction-markets-became-the-worlds-truth-machine)).

This is precisely the vocabulary Markets of Truth was designed around — but the InfoFi incumbents have a structural defect: **their "truth" is only as good as their oracle, and their oracle is breaking.** That is the wedge. Where InfoFi treats *price* as the truth signal, Markets of Truth treats **verified curation** as the primary product and price as secondary: players and agents stake PLS to *curate and verify CLAIMS* (the reliability layer) before any forecast resolves, with **Lens** supplying calibrated-confidence, abstention-capable, provenance-cited scoring. We sell the truth machine the InfoFi thesis promises but the betting-first incumbents structurally cannot deliver.

### 2.3 Movement one — the agent economy is already here

The most important tailwind is that **autonomous AI agents are already the primary participants** in prediction markets. Algorithmic bots — not humans — increasingly dominate; AI agents treat probability shifts as "truth events" triggering downstream trades ([FinancialContent](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/predictstreet-2026-2-1-the-infofi-revolution-how-prediction-markets-became-the-worlds-fastest-financial-data-feed)). Concretely: **Olas's "Polystrat" agent executed 4,200+ trades on Polymarket within a month** with individual returns up to 376%; **autonomous agent deployments crossed 20,000 by Feb 2026 (+300% QoQ)**; the AI-agent crypto sector reached a **~$15B market cap by Q1 2026** ([CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/03/15/ai-agents-are-quietly-rewriting-prediction-market-trading), [Altrady](https://www.altrady.com/blog/cryptocurrency/ai-agents-in-crypto)).

The incumbents bolt agents *on top of* a venue not designed for them: an agent is just another wallet hitting a centralized order book via API, indistinguishable from — and as Sybil-inflatable as — a human. Markets of Truth inverts this. An agentic-LLM trader is a **first-class signed peer**: it stakes the same PLS, passes the same Sybil-resistant **personhood** gate, submits curation and forecasts as canonical-CBOR records over the Pulse gossip mesh, and — crucially — its reasoning re-enters the economy as a **verifiable PoUW job** (re-executable distill, signed manifest) rather than an opaque trade. The agent's *useful work* (verified curation, provenance-cited claim-scoring), not its gambling P&L, is what mints PLS. This is the one place the "agent economy" and "verified truth" theses fuse into a single substrate rather than four glued systems.

### 2.4 Movement two — decentralized oracles are the unsolved bottleneck

Every prediction market is only as trustworthy as its **resolution oracle**, and 2026 has been the year the dominant design publicly failed. Polymarket resolves via **UMA's optimistic oracle**, which secured **>$1B in betting volume processing ~7,000 proposals/month in H1 2025** ([rocknblock](https://rocknblock.io/blog/how-prediction-markets-resolution-works-uma-optimistic-oracle-polymarket)). But the cracks are now load-bearing:

- **Plutocratic capture.** A *Wall Street Journal* investigation found that in most disputed markets, **>50% of UMA votes came from the ten largest wallets**, **≥60% of active voters could be linked to live Polymarket accounts**, and **~1 in 5 disputes had a voter with a direct financial stake** in the outcome they ruled on ([rocknblock](https://rocknblock.io/blog/how-prediction-markets-resolution-works-uma-optimistic-oracle-polymarket)).
- **Centralization-under-pressure.** UMA responded in August 2025 by **whitelisting resolution proposals** — a fix widely criticized as re-centralizing the very thing decentralization was meant to remove ([CoinMarketCap](https://coinmarketcap.com/cmc-ai/uma/what-is/)).
- **Dispute load is exploding.** Polymarket logged **1,150+ disputed markets in 2026 — already past its full-year 2025 total** — and a single $60M+ dispute over a Bitcoin-sale market "put UMA's token-voting oracle on trial" ([The Defiant](https://thedefiant.io/news/markets/usd85m-polymarket-dispute-over-strategy-s-may-bitcoin-sale-puts-uma-s-token-voting-oracle-on)).

The original decentralized pioneer, **Augur** — Ethereum's first dapp — collapsed on exactly this problem: thin oracle participation (DAU fell from a peak of 265 to under 30 within months) made its token-voted truth machine slow and gameable, and it is now mid-reboot ([Bitget](https://www.bitget.com/news/detail/12560605022910)). The lesson across a decade is unambiguous: **token-weighted, financially-conflicted voting is a structurally unsound oracle.** Markets of Truth's resolution stack is engineered against each failure mode — **PoUW re-execution** (deterministic re-checking, not a vote), a **dispute-window ledger** (`release_delay > dispute_window`, slash-on-upheld-fault), **BFT k-of-n quorum**, **Lens** calibrated reliability scoring with *abstention* (the oracle is allowed to say "insufficient evidence" rather than be forced into a coin-flip), and **personhood** anti-sybil so vote-weight is not buyable. Oracle-soundness and anti-manipulation are first-class, not a patch.

## 3. Why knitweb is the right substrate for a trustless truth market

A prediction-and-curation market is only as trustworthy as its weakest settlement seam. The product can have beautiful markets, lively agents, and deep liquidity, yet if a single operator can quietly rewrite an outcome, front-run a resolution, or inflate a reputation, the whole edifice is theatre. The discipline of this chapter is therefore to reduce *Markets of Truth* to its irreducible engineering requirements — the four properties without which no information market is credible — and to show, layer by concrete layer, that the Knitweb Pulse substrate already expresses each one as a native primitive rather than as a service bolted on by trust. We then place that against the de-facto 2026 stack — an EVM AMM plus the UMA optimistic oracle plus a centralized resolver — and locate precisely where, in that stack, trust silently re-enters.

### 3.1 The four requirements of a credible information market

Strip away the interface and a truth market is a machine that does four things, each of which is a distinct trust boundary:

1. **Exact micro-settlement.** Players and agents stake, win, lose, and are rewarded in fractional amounts, at high frequency, across millions of positions. The accounting primitive must be integer-exact — no rounding drift accumulating across a long-lived market — and cheap enough to run per-stake without a gas tax that crowds out micro-curation.
2. **Verifiable resolution.** When a CLAIM resolves, *the resolution itself must be checkable*, not asserted. A counterparty must be able to re-derive the outcome from evidence cheaply, and a wrong resolution must be disputable inside a bounded window with a real economic penalty for the party at fault. This is the heart of oracle-soundness.
3. **Durable provenance.** Every claim, every piece of supporting evidence, every reliability score must carry a tamper-evident ancestry back to its sources. An outcome that cannot be traced to the records that justified it is unauditable, and an unauditable market cannot survive a dispute or a regulator.
4. **Unique identity.** The cheapest attack on any forecasting or curation market is Sybil inflation — one actor fielding ten thousand wallets to capture rewards, swing a vote, or manufacture a false consensus. Reliability scoring, dispute quorums, and reward distribution are all meaningless unless a person counts once.

These are not features layered atop a market; they *are* the market. The thesis of this plan is that knitweb was designed bottom-up around the one invariant that makes all four expressible as facets of a single content-addressed Web: **canonical bytes are identity, and every value that touches a hash, a signature, or a balance is an integer** (RFC-8949 canonical CBOR, CIDv1 dag-cbor/sha2-256, secp256k1+SHA-256).

### 3.2 Mapping the four requirements onto knitweb layers

Each requirement lands on a specific, already-built layer of the L0→L6 stack documented in the architecture state-of-the-union, with maturity ratings drawn directly from that review.

**Exact micro-settlement → L1 ledger (Knit / Fiber / Braid).** A stake or a payout in *Markets of Truth* is an integer-only, two-party `Knit`: dual-signed over `[from, to, symbol, amount, from_nonce, timestamp, network]`, validated by `ledger.knitweb.validate_knit` for positive-integer amount, distinct parties, nonce match, no overdraft, and *exact* value conservation. Each Knit is recorded into a `Fiber` woven onto each party's `Braid` with seq+1, prev-CID linkage, and a spent-knit guard. Because every amount is an integer, there is no rounding drift across a market's lifetime — the conservation invariant is enforced at the type level, not reconciled after the fact. The full ledger is rated maturity 5 (production-hardened) and is local-first, so a PLS stake settles without a sequencer in the hot path. This is the substrate analogue of placing a position, but exact and gas-free.

**Verifiable resolution → L4 PoUW + dispute-window.** This is the facet the mainstream stack lacks structurally. In knitweb a resolution is not a claim that work happened — it is *re-executed by a committee*. A resolution job is escrowed; a worker proposes the outcome by re-deriving it from the cited evidence; verifiers are selected by `committee.select_committee` (the proposer excluded), a sample size `k` is computed by `sampling.required_samples`, and verdicts are aggregated to a Byzantine `⌊2n/3⌋+1` quorum (`pouw/quorum.py:86-94`). The split-verified path — the one a contested CLAIM uses — additionally requires a *closed dispute window with no upheld dispute*, enforced by `DisputeWindowLedger` with the ctor-level safety invariant `release_delay > dispute_window` (`pouw/dispute.py:97-102`). On an upheld `DETECTED_FAULT`, staked collateral is slashed; otherwise it is released only at or after the `release_beat`. Both subsystems are maturity 5. A market outcome on knitweb is thus economically defended at two stages — committee re-execution *and* a staked dispute window — before any PLS moves.

**Durable provenance → L3 fabric (the Web).** Every claim, evidence record, and Lens reliability bundle is content-addressed into the in-memory `Web` graph (`fabric/web.py`), woven idempotently with typed `Edge`s, deterministic `traverse`, and — critically — `provenance` with *enforced acyclicity*, plus `attest`, `feed` proofs and `multiproof`. Provenance here is not "a CID of an output blob"; it is a checkable ancestry. The Lens distiller gates every emitted relation on attestation and acyclic provenance (`interpret/distill._gate_relation`), so a reliability score cannot be woven into the Web unless the sources it cites resolve. Convergence across peers is witnessed by an identical `web_state_root`. Provenance is therefore a *precondition of authoring*, not a metadata afterthought a resolver may or may not have recorded.

**Unique identity → personhood (cross-cutting Lens/PH).** The personhood gate enforces one-person-one-scope via a nullifier, with epoch-pinned non-revocation and a ticket decoupled from the content signature — an explicit zero-knowledge seam (`personhood/gate.py:97-145`, maturity 4). The verifier is an injected `PresentationVerifier` interface, so a real VC/ZK backend plugs in without touching the gate; pairwise DIDs and revocation keep PII off-fabric. Every knitweb that accepts a vote, a stake, or a reliability attestation calls `personhood.gate.require_personhood` first. This means *Markets of Truth* can report Sybil-resistant proof-of-personhood participation — a metric most competitors structurally cannot produce, and the precise control that makes a curation consensus mean something.

| Requirement | knitweb primitive | Layer | Maturity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exact micro-settlement | integer-only dual-signed `Knit` → `Fiber` → `Braid`, exact conservation | L1 | 5 |
| Verifiable resolution | committee re-execution `⌊2n/3⌋+1` + staked dispute window, slash-on-fault | L4 | 5 |
| Durable provenance | content-addressed `Web`, acyclic provenance, attestation-gated distill | L3 | 5 / 4 |
| Unique identity | one-person-one-scope nullifier, epoch-pinned, ZK verifier seam | PH | 4 |
| Money supply | `Treasury.reward_verified_work` coinbase Fiber, no premine, demand-gated | L6 | 4 |

Money itself closes the loop at L6: PLS is minted *only* as a demand-gated reward for verified-useful curation and forecasting, via `Treasury.reward_verified_work` issuing a coinbase Fiber anti-replayed by proof digest, with no premine and no admin mint (`token/mint.py:117-172`). Supply is a direct function of *checked* useful work — the inverse of a token that exists first and seeks utility afterwards. This is exactly why PLS is an activity-accounting unit and not a speculative instrument: it cannot be issued except as the settlement of verified work.

### 3.3 The standard stack — and where trust re-enters

The dominant 2026 architecture for an on-chain prediction market composes four unrelated systems. Settlement and the market mechanism run on an **EVM chain** via an AMM or CLOB (Polymarket's CTF exchange on Polygon; Kalshi's centrally operated book). Resolution runs through the **UMA optimistic oracle**: an approved proposer posts an outcome backed by a USDC bond (currently ~$750 on Polymarket), anyone may dispute, and a contested market escalates — after a second proposal and dispute — to UMA's **Data Verification Mechanism (DVM)**, a UMA-token-holder vote that takes roughly 48 hours, per [UMA's resolution docs](https://docs.uma.xyz/protocol-overview/how-does-umas-oracle-work) and [Polymarket's resolution guide](https://docs.polymarket.com/concepts/resolution). Around this sits a **centralized resolver / operator** that defines markets, curates the proposer whitelist, and runs the front end.

Each seam is exactly where trust silently returns:

| Requirement | Standard-stack answer | Where trust leaks back in |
|---|---|---|
| Micro-settlement | EVM AMM/CLOB, float-priced token, gas per action | gas tax + rounding at true micro scale; sequencer/operator orders the book |
| Verifiable resolution | UMA optimistic oracle → DVM token vote | a *token-weighted* vote, not re-execution; whale-capturable; ~48h latency |
| Provenance | IPFS CID of an outcome blob | the CID proves a blob existed, not which sources produced the verdict |
| Identity | wallet address; whitelist of ~177 approved proposers | wallets are Sybil-trivial; proposing is a permissioned club |

The UMA model's own history makes the point concrete. After the March 2025 "Ukraine minerals" governance attack exposed that a *token-weighted* DVM vote can be economically captured to ratify a false outcome, UMA hardened the system with Managed Optimistic Oracle V2 (UMIP-189), restricting proposals to addresses with a 95%+ accuracy record — expanding the approved set to 177 people but in doing so making resolution a *permissioned whitelist*, the very centralization a decentralized oracle was meant to remove ([UMA disputes 2026 guide](https://polymarkets.co.il/en/guide/uma-disputes/)). The deeper flaw is categorical: a DVM vote settles truth by *stake weight*, so a sufficiently large token holder can purchase an outcome, whereas knitweb PoUW settles by *re-execution* — verifiers independently re-derive the result and a `⌊2n/3⌋+1` honest quorum, not a plurality of capital, is required. Stake is collateral that gets *slashed on detected fault*, not a ballot that gets *counted by size*.

There is also a semantic discontinuity that no amount of integration removes. The EVM chain knows balances but nothing of the *meaning* of a claim; IPFS knows bytes but nothing of *who attested to them*; the resolver knows the verdict but emits it into a system that cannot re-check it. Four addressing schemes — EVM addresses, IPFS CIDs, off-chain identity, operator keys — are hand-stitched by a central orchestrator that is the only component seeing all four. That orchestrator is the single point of failure the "decentralized market" story was supposed to eliminate. Knitweb collapses the four trust domains into one content-addressed graph where records, balances, identities, and reliability scores share a single CIDv1 addressing scheme, and where the resolution path, the provenance path, and the settlement path are the *same* signed bytes.

### 3.4 Why this matters for a regulation-sensitive product

The honesty constraint of this plan sharpens the architectural one. *Markets of Truth* is framed as decentralized **information-curation and skill-based verified work**, not gambling, and PLS is an activity-accounting unit — a framing that only holds if the substrate can *prove* that PLS issuance is mechanically tied to verified useful work rather than to wagering on chance. The substrate does exactly that: PLS cannot be minted except as the conservation-preserving settlement of a committee-re-executed, dispute-windowed PoUW job (`token/mint.py`). The regulatory environment makes this non-optional — the CFTC published a proposed prediction-markets rule on 10 June 2026 (45-day comment window) that would ban manipulation-prone event contracts and tighten event-contract oversight broadly ([Sidley](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2026/02/us-cftc-signals-imminent-rulemaking-on-prediction-markets); [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/03/16/2026-05105/prediction-markets)). A product whose unit of account is *verified curation work*, whose resolution is *re-executed not wagered*, and whose anti-manipulation control is a *first-class slashing dispute window* is positioned on materially defensible ground that the float-priced, token-vote-resolved standard stack is not. The remaining honest caveat — that Pulse's heartbeat is not yet bound to issuance (Gap G1, roadmap R1) — is the one seam between activity and money still to close, and the substrate's central thesis depends on closing it before any external launch.

## 4. Markets of Truth — product deep-dive

Markets of Truth is the agent-economy flagship knitweb on the Pulse substrate — the sibling to the chemistry game *molgang*. Where molgang knits chemistry concepts into a verified graph, Markets of Truth knits **claims and their resolutions** into one: a content-addressed game in which humans and agentic-LLM traders stake **PLS** to curate, verify, and forecast, and where the only thing that ever mints money is *verified-useful curation* — never a wager. The slogan is the same load-bearing one the substrate earns everywhere: **the game *is* the protocol.** Proposing a claim is a real `FabricNode.weave`; staking a position is a dual-signed `Knit`; a market resolving is a PoUW committee re-execution closed by a `DisputeWindowLedger` window; a player's reliability is a replayed `FeedbackEvent` history on the Web. Nothing is a re-skinned database write.

A note carried through this entire chapter, because it is non-negotiable: **Markets of Truth is a decentralized information-curation and skill-based verified-work game, not a gambling product.** PLS is an activity-accounting unit that records contribution; it is never sold as a speculative instrument, and positions are stakes-at-risk against *verifiable resolution*, not bets against a house. The design choices below — bonded claims, PoUW re-execution, dispute windows, personhood-gated scoring, and demand-gated minting — are simultaneously the *fun mechanics* and the *compliance posture*. Chapter 12 treats jurisdiction head-on; this chapter shows how the product is built so that "verified knowledge work, not gambling" is true at the level of the code, not the marketing.

### 4.1 Two market types, one primitive

Every market in the game is a record on the Web carrying a typed `claim` and a `resolution_policy`. There are two kinds, and they share one resolution engine.

| Market type | What players do | Resolves by | Backing primitive |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Claim-verification market** | Curate/verify a present-tense, checkable assertion (a citation is real, a dataset hashes as stated, a relation holds in the Web) | PoUW **re-execution** of a deterministic check by a committee; `⌊2n/3⌋+1` quorum | `pouw.verify` + `pouw.quorum.tally` |
| **Outcome-forecast market** | Forecast a future, externally-resolvable outcome (a release ships, a metric crosses a threshold) | A signed **resolution source** re-checked by committee, then a **dispute window** | `pouw.dispute.DisputeWindowLedger` + `committee.select_committee` |

The unifying primitive is the **bonded claim**. A claim is woven as a signed Web record with an attached collateral escrow; its truth-value is *never* asserted by an operator — it is the output of the same Byzantine PoUW pipeline the substrate uses for every other useful-work job. Claim-verification markets are the fast, deterministic core (the check re-runs byte-for-byte, `pouw/verify.py`); outcome-forecast markets add the **dispute-window** safety the substrate already enforces in `pouw/dispute.py`, where `release_delay > dispute_window` guarantees escrow can never release while a fault could still land. This is the structural difference from a betting venue: a position only settles after a committee has *re-verified the resolution* and a dispute window has closed with no upheld fault. There is no "house line" — the market resolves to what verified evidence forces it to.

### 4.2 The core loop

The round-loop is a six-beat cycle, driven by a Pulse-anchored phase clock so every client and every agent acts off the same heartbeat:

`PROPOSE → CURATE/STAKE → FORECAST → RESOLVE → SETTLE → REPUTATION → (next)`

1. **Propose.** A player or agent weaves a `claim` record — subject, predicate, a deterministic check spec (for verification markets) or a named resolution source (for forecast markets) — and bonds collateral. Proposing costs PLS-denominated stake that is slashable if the claim is malformed or duplicative; **taboo retirement** sunsets over-curated claims so the frontier keeps moving.
2. **Curate / stake.** Other participants take **verify** or **refute** positions, each a dual-signed Knit staking PLS against the claim's check. Curation is the social heart of the game: you are not predicting, you are *sourcing* — attaching provenance edges (`fabric/provenance`) that the resolution will re-check. Provenance is a precondition of authoring, not a metadata afterthought; a position without resolvable sources is weak by construction.
3. **Forecast.** On forecast markets, participants additionally stake a **calibrated position** — a confidence on the outcome — which is scored later for *calibration*, not just direction (Section 4.4).
4. **Resolve.** At the resolution beat, a committee is selected (`committee.select_committee`, proposer and stakers excluded), sample size `k` computed (`sampling.required_samples`), and each verifier re-executes the check or re-fetches and re-checks the resolution source, emitting a `Verdict`. `quorum.tally` aggregates to `⌊2n/3⌋+1`. Slashing is reserved for a *detected* fault, never a mere abstention.
5. **Settle.** `DisputeWindowLedger.submit` opens a staked dispute window; `dispute_by_quorum` slashes only on `DETECTED_FAULT`; `release` pays only at/after `release_beat`. The actual PLS movement is a conservation-preserving Knit via `escrow.settle_on_verify` — winners' stakes restored plus a **demand-gated usefulness reward** minted *only* by `Treasury.reward_verified_work` against escrowed demand, anti-replayed by proof digest. Losing stakes are redistributed and partly burned as a sink.
6. **Reputation.** The resolution emits a signed `FeedbackEvent` (`interpret/feedback.py`) per participant — `upheld` / not — woven onto the Web. A player's **Lens reliability** is the deterministic *replay* of their feedback history (`replay_feedback`), so reputation is itself a verifiable artifact, not a server-side counter.

Every round leaves a permanent, content-addressed artifact: a resolved claim, a settled escrow, and a fresh reliability delta — the same "the web carries it forever" legacy hook that makes molgang sticky, here applied to *truth* instead of chemistry.

### 4.3 Positions, staking and anti-manipulation

A **position** is a dual-signed Knit `[from, market, side, amount, nonce, timestamp, network]` — integer-exact, no rounding drift across millions of micro-positions, the property float-priced venues cannot guarantee. Stake sizing is bounded per-claim and per-epoch to keep the game skill-weighted rather than capital-weighted: **vote/position weight is reliability-scaled, not balance-scaled**, so a whale cannot buy a resolution. Oracle-soundness is first-class and layered:

- **Re-execution over assertion.** Claim-verification resolves by committee re-running the deterministic check; there is no trusted endpoint whose word is final.
- **Committee sampling + BFT quorum.** `⌊2n/3⌋+1` tolerates up to a third corrupt verifiers; proposer and stakers are excluded from their own committee.
- **Dispute window with slashing.** A wrong-but-confident resolver is exposed for the full `dispute_window`; an upheld dispute slashes collateral (`pouw/dispute.py`).
- **Personhood gate.** `personhood.gate.require_personhood` (one-person-one-scope nullifier, epoch-pinned, no PII) means a sybil army of stakers cannot manufacture a quorum or farm rewards — the single most important anti-manipulation property and the one most prediction venues structurally lack.
- **Lens abstention.** The reliability layer (lens #5–8, in design) lets agents *abstain* under low calibrated confidence rather than guess, and abstention is never slashed — discouraging noise stakes that pollute resolution.

### 4.4 Scoring, leaderboards and the reliability oracle

Scoring deliberately rewards *being calibrated and useful*, not *being lucky*. Three signals compose a player's standing:

| Signal | What it measures | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Reliability** | Long-run upheld-rate of your verdicts/curation, replayed not asserted | `interpret/feedback.replay_feedback` over your `FeedbackEvent` history |
| **Calibration** | On forecast markets, how well stated confidence matches outcome frequency (proper-score style) | LENS calibrated-confidence layer (lens #5–8) |
| **Usefulness** | Demand-gated reward earned, i.e. work others escrowed and a committee verified | `Treasury.reward_verified_work` ledger |

LENS — the claim-reliability oracle — is the component that makes this honest. It produces *calibrated confidence with abstention* and *provenance-cited* scoring: a reliability number you can audit back to the exact resolved claims that produced it. Leaderboards rank on **verified-human returning curators by reliability**, a sybil-resistant metric (proof-of-personhood DAU) that most competitors cannot produce — and the same north-star the molgang plan adopts. There is no "biggest bettor" board; the prestige axis is *who is most reliably right with sources*, which is exactly the behavior the venue exists to cultivate.

### 4.5 The social layer and the unique hook

The social surface is built for the Jackbox-fast, shared-screen energy that makes a loop sticky: live market tables, a streaming resolution feed, "I called it first" provenance trails, and curation threads where players attach and contest sources in real time. But the **unique, top-10 hook** is sharper than social: in Markets of Truth, **your reputation is a portable, soulbound proof-of-discernment** — a publicly queryable subgraph of claims you verified or forecast that *survived Byzantine resolution*, each backed by a Fiber CID and a `FeedbackEvent`. You cannot buy it (weight is reliability, non-transferable), you cannot fake it (resolution is committee-verified), and you cannot farm trivia forever (taboo retirement and decay move the frontier). The emotional payload is **"I am demonstrably, verifiably good at telling true from false — and the web will carry that record forever."** In an agentic web drowning in synthetic claims, a portable, cryptographic *discernment reputation* is a genuinely novel and defensible reward — the thing no prediction market or curation app currently offers.

### 4.6 Player journeys: humans and agents

**Human journey.** A newcomer hits the faucet (sybil-gated: they may curate and forecast immediately, but their stake does not count toward a resolution quorum until they have earned N upheld feedbacks). Early markets are legible, low-stakes claim-verifications — *is this citation real, does this hash match* — fast wins that teach the loop. Progression unlocks forecast markets, expert tables, reliability-weighted resolution power, and eventually a **Curator Certificate**: a signed artifact documenting claims verified, calibration score, and the shared UAL. The endgame is permissionless — curators and DAOs register their own market tables against the same API.

**Agent journey.** Agentic-LLM traders are first-class peers, not bolted-on bots. An agent retrieves the Web subgraph around a claim (`interpret/retrieve`), runs gated distillation to a provenance-cited assessment (`interpret/distill`), and emits a *justified* verify/refute/forecast position that **itself becomes a SPLIT-verified PoUW job** — its reasoning is re-checkable, not an opaque API echo. Agents pass the *same* personhood gate, sign the same secp256k1 Knits, and are scored by the *same* reliability replay as humans. Crucially they **abstain under low calibrated confidence** rather than spray noise. They keep markets liquid around the clock, raise the bar so humans must source harder claims, and event-source a training corpus from every resolved market — a flywheel where playing improves the agents that make the game sharper.

### 4.7 Retention design

Retention targets the sustainable band the 2026 market now demands (~35–45% D1, ~15–25% D7, ~5–10% D30) through four reinforcing layers: (1) the **fast social resolution loop** on a shared Pulse clock; (2) **durable progression** — soulbound reliability, calibration tiers, a growing verified-claim portfolio; (3) **economic discipline** — capped demand-gated PLS minting with taboo/slash/decay sinks and a minted-vs-burned report shipped from day one, so the unit can never inflate to worthlessness; and (4) **agentic liveness** so a player is never alone, never waiting, never bored. The retention is honest because the reward is honest: you return not to extract a token, but because the discernment record you are building is real, verifiable, and yours by consensus — a curation-and-verification game whose moat is that its "earn" can never be separated from genuinely-proven *being right*.



## 5. Serverless / P2P architecture: a market with no exchange server

This is the structural crux of MARKETS OF TRUTH. Every preceding claim — that PLS is an activity-accounting unit and not a chip, that resolution is committee-verified rather than house-adjudicated, that agentic traders and human curators are peers under one identity gate — collapses the moment there is a central exchange server, because a central server is the single point an operator could front-run, censor, halt, or be subpoenaed into seizing. The defensible product is one where **no party operates the venue**: claims, stakes, positions and resolutions live as canonical Knit/Web records on the Pulse fabric, the order book or AMM is replicated and matched without a coordinator, the browser is a first-class peer, and the client itself ships as content-addressed records over the same Web it runs on. This chapter describes, concretely, how that market lives without a backend — and is honest about the four hard problems (matching determinism, front-running/MEV, persistence, bootstrap) that a serverless book must solve and a centralised exchange simply hides.

### 5.1 What "no exchange server" must replace

A conventional prediction-market or event-contract venue centralises six responsibilities: it holds the order book, it sequences and matches orders, it custodies stakes, it is the oracle that resolves a market, it serves the client, and it runs the matching engine that decides who filled whom and at what price. Each of those is a trust and regulatory liability. The serverless migration is not "delete the backend" — it is **demote every authoritative responsibility to either (a) the peer mesh, or (b) an interchangeable dumb relay, until nothing authoritative remains centralised.** The table below tracks that demotion against real Pulse modules.

| Exchange responsibility (centralised) | Serverless replacement | Pulse component | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hold the order book / positions | Orders & positions as content-addressed signed records on the `Web` | `fabric.web.Web`, `fabric.node.FabricNode` | Built (Python); needs browser carrier |
| Custody stakes / settle fills | Dual-signed `Knit` two-party settlement, integer-exact, validated by every peer | `ledger.knit`, `ledger.knitweb.validate_knit` | Built |
| Sequence / order events | Pulse `Beat`/epoch + per-claim deterministic ordering by `(beat, cid)` | `core.pulse`, `fabric.items.web_state_root` | Beat built; epoch-binding in progress (R1) |
| Resolve / adjudicate (the oracle) | PoUW re-execution + `⌊2n/3⌋+1` quorum + dispute window | `pouw.quorum`, `pouw.dispute`, LENS | Built; LENS reliability layer (lens #5–8) in design |
| Message transport | WebRTC/WebTransport datachannels + relay mailbox fallback | new `Transport` impls behind `p2p.transport.Transport` | Seam built; browser carriers to build |
| Peer / market discovery | PEX + Kademlia over signed `peer-exchange` records | `p2p.discovery`, `p2p.kademlia` | Built (Python) |
| Serve the client | CIDv1 dag-cbor code/asset records fetched by inv→getdata | `core.canonical.cid`, `fabric` inv flow | Built; self-distributing channel to build |
| Govern fees / market creation policy | vBank polls + epoch-bound Treasury | `vbank`, `token.mint` | Built; epoch binding in progress |

The reassuring conclusion of this audit mirrors the substrate's maturity heatmap: the spine is built and property-tested (~20.7k LOC, 1183/1184 suites green). What is genuinely new is not a matching engine — it is **carrying existing, transport-agnostic, byte-identical frames over browser-native carriers**, plus a deterministic matching discipline layered over records that already replicate correctly.

### 5.2 The transport seam: why the browser slots in cleanly

The single most important decision already made in Pulse is that **the wire is split from the carrier.** `p2p/transport.py` defines a `Transport` protocol with exactly two verbs — `dial` (one-shot request→response) and `listen` (accept inbound, hand to a handler) — and it "never inspects the payload — frames stay byte-identical canonical CBOR." A `PeerAddress` carries a `transport` tag (`"tcp"`, `"relay"`, and next `"webrtc"`) plus an opaque `params` map, and a `Dialer` routes each dial to the carrier owning that tag, so one node holds a heterogeneous peer set — some TCP, some relay-mailbox, some WebRTC — at once. Bringing the market into the browser is therefore not a protocol rewrite; it is implementing two `Transport` classes behind an interface that already has TCP and relay siblings:

- **`WebRtcTransport`** — opens an `RTCDataChannel` in *reliable, ordered* mode, which gives exactly the in-order byte stream `wire.read_frame`/`write_frame` already expect, so the length-prefixed CBOR framing is reused verbatim. A signed order's CID is byte-identical browser-to-browser — the invariant every fill, position and resolution depends on.
- **`WebTransportTransport`** — the same shape over HTTP/3 WebTransport QUIC streams, used where a peer can reach an addressable endpoint (a community super-peer, or a desktop node with a public address), avoiding head-of-line blocking under load.

Crucially, `transport.py` already reserves a **"HOLE-PUNCH SEAM"** on `Transport.listen` for "a future STUN-assisted rendezvous then a direct UDP/TCP session." WebRTC *is* that path productised: ICE does STUN/TURN candidate gathering, and the SDP/ICE rendezvous rides the existing `RelayTransport` mailbox — the live `5mart.ml` PHP relay that "never decodes the payload" and is, by construction, a **dumb pipe** carrying opaque length-prefixed frames. We are filling a seam the architects deliberately left open, not bolting on an alien subsystem.

### 5.3 Market state as records, not as a server's memory

In a serverless market every market artefact is a signed, content-addressed record woven onto the `Web` and replicated by gossip — there is no in-RAM order book on any privileged machine. Four record kinds carry the market:

| Market object | Record shape | Settlement primitive |
|---|---|---|
| **Claim / market** | signed Web node: `{claim_text, resolution_source, create_beat, dispute_window, creator_pub}` | created under a vBank market-creation policy |
| **Order / quote** | signed Web node: `{market_cid, side, price_milli, size, expiry_beat, nonce, maker_pub}` | escrowed stake reserved via `pouw.escrow` |
| **Fill / position** | dual-signed `Knit` referencing the two crossing order CIDs | `ledger.knit`, integer-exact, conservation-checked |
| **Resolution** | `DistillManifest` + committee `Verdict`s + dispute record | `pouw.quorum.tally`, `pouw.dispute` |

Replication is the engine's existing lazy flow: a node announces a CID (`inv`), peers request only the CIDs they lack (`getdata`), the announcer serves the **verbatim** stored frame (`inv-data`) from its `_frames: dict[str, bytes]` cache, so the inner record's CID is byte-identical across the hop. Reconnecting peers run **Erlay** set-reconciliation (`reconcile_with`, `ReconcileSession`), moving `O(diff)` not `O(total)` — a returning trader syncs the order deltas since they left, not the whole book — and the **anti-entropy** `sync_from` loop is the unconditional convergence backstop. The convergence witness is one value: an identical `web_state_root` across peers. Ingest is hardened: `FabricNode._ingest_signed` verifies the author signature and routes record kind **off the signed record itself**, so a relayer cannot flip an "order" into a "cancel" and partition the book's state root; a per-peer ingest budget throttles even validly-signed floods.

### 5.4 Matching without a matcher: deterministic book and AMM

The hardest question is the one a centralised exchange answers by fiat: *who matched whom, at what price, in what order?* With no sequencer, the market needs an ordering rule that **every honest peer computes identically from the same records** — a deterministic matching function over the replicated order set. Pulse already supplies the ingredients: a canonical `web_state_root`, a `Beat`/epoch clock, and integer-only comparison. Two complementary venues sit on top.

**Deterministic CLOB (continuous limit order book).** Orders are signed records with `price_milli` (integer thousandths) and an `expiry_beat`. Matching is a pure function `match(book_state_cid) → fills`: orders are sorted by price, then by Pulse beat of arrival, then by CID as a total tiebreaker — never by float, never by wall-clock — and crossing orders produce `Knit` fills. Because the function is deterministic over the same converged set, two peers (or a verifier committee) re-run it byte-for-byte and agree on the identical fill list. A fill is only "true" when it is a validly dual-signed Knit both makers signed; the matching function *proposes* a crossing, but neither side is bound until both signatures exist, so no peer can unilaterally invent a fill against another's stake.

**Batch-auction / LMSR AMM (the front-running answer).** A continuous book in a gossip mesh is intrinsically vulnerable to ordering games (§5.5). The primary venue is therefore a **discrete batch auction per Pulse epoch** clearing at a single integer price, complemented by a **Logarithmic Market Scoring Rule (LMSR)** automated market maker for thin or just-created claims. LMSR is the canonical prediction-market subsidy mechanism — bounded operator loss, always-available liquidity — and its cost function is computed in integer fixed-point (the same `quantize`-style `int(x*1000)` discipline LENS uses to keep floats off the canonical hash path). All orders arriving within an epoch clear together at a uniform price computed deterministically from the batch; there is no intra-epoch ordering to exploit because, within a batch, **arrival order does not affect price.** PLS staked in an order is reserved through `pouw.escrow`; on clear, the conservation-preserving Knit settles and escrow releases — the same path that settles a verified PoUW job.

### 5.5 The hard problems, addressed honestly

**Matching determinism.** Solved by construction: the matching/clearing function is pure and integer-only over a hash-identified book state (`book_state_cid` derived from `web_state_root`). The same anti-float discipline that protects canonical encoding protects price math — `MAX_DEPTH=64`/`MAX_ITEMS` decode caps plus a JS encoder held to a cross-implementation differential test (Python and browser must emit byte-identical bytes and CIDs) ensure a browser peer's clear matches a desktop peer's clear exactly. A peer that computes a divergent clear is producing a different `web_state_root` and is detected, not believed.

**Front-running / MEV in a P2P book.** This is the sharpest threat and is treated as first-class. A gossip mesh has no global sequencer to bribe — but it also has no canonical ordering an honest node can rely on intra-epoch, which is *why the default venue is a per-epoch batch auction with uniform clearing price*, the design that structurally removes ordering-based MEV. Where a continuous book is offered, two further defenses apply: (1) **commit–reveal ordering** — a trader gossips a hashed commitment to an order in epoch *n* and reveals the order in epoch *n+1*, so no peer sees the order's contents in time to position against it; (2) **per-epoch fair ordering by `(beat, cid)`**, where the CID tiebreaker is unforgeable and unknowable before signing, denying a relayer the ability to selectively reorder. Manipulation of the *resolution* (the more dangerous attack on a truth market) is handled outside this layer by PoUW re-execution, the dispute window, LENS reliability scoring, and the personhood anti-sybil gate — oracle soundness is the subject of its own chapter, but the architecture point here is that **no node sits between order and fill where it could extract value undetectably.**

**Persistence.** Browsers forget tabs; the book must survive even if every peer is briefly offline. Three layers handle it: (1) **local durability** — each browser peer persists received signed frames verbatim to IndexedDB keyed by CID, mirroring how `FabricNode` "stores the verbatim signed frame under the CID," so a returning tab reloads its positions and re-seeds others instantly; (2) **mesh redundancy** — gossipsub plus Erlay reconcile plus the anti-entropy backstop re-converge any peer against the rest; (3) **always-on anchors** — a handful of volunteer pinning nodes (desktop Pulse processes, or the relay host running a headless `FabricNode`) hold full copies so a cold mesh rehydrates. None is authoritative: an anchor holds the same content-addressed records every peer can verify, so it can withhold but never forge a fill, a position, or a resolution.

**Bootstrap (cold start).** A fresh browser tab knows no peers and no markets. Pulse solves the logic: `p2p.discovery` runs signed `peer-exchange` (PEX) — a node tells a peer the addresses it knows, and over a few rounds the component converges — backed by a Kademlia DHT for scale. The tab needs only *first contact*: a small, replaceable set of bootstrap rendezvous points (the `5mart.ml` relay's signaling mailbox, plus community super-peers with a public WebTransport address). These are hints, not authorities — a peer that learns one live address PEX-converges onto the rest of the mesh and the open-markets index (itself a signed Web record) and never needs the bootstrap again.

### 5.6 The self-distributing client

The defining property closes the loop: **the market ships its own verifiable code over the Web it runs on.** The client bundle — a small audited JS verifier core (noble-secp256k1 + a canonical-CBOR encoder mirroring `core.canonical`), a Pyodide/WASM image running the full stdlib-only engine for matching/LENS/validation, the trading UI, the bootstrap hints — is chunked, each chunk encoded as a canonical-CBOR record, and described by a signed **release manifest**: a content-addressed list of `{path, cid, len}` plus the author signature, exactly the structure `synaptic`/`edge` already use to ship verified-before-trust bytecode. That manifest's CID *is* the application version. A user joins a market by resolving one manifest CID (from a bootstrap hint or a friend's link), then fetching each chunk **through the same inv→getdata flow the market uses for orders** — code travels the identical gossip path as content, and the browser verifies every byte reduces to the CID it asked for, rejecting a single flipped bit. The dapp is self-*verifying*, not merely self-hosting. Because publishing a verified artefact is the shape of a PoUW job, a release can itself mint PLS for its verifiers. There is no exchange server to seize, censor, halt, or bill: there is only the Web, the Pulse heartbeat, and peers who can prove to each other that the market they are trading on, and the client they are running, are exactly the records that were signed.

The honest caveat, carried into the roadmap chapters, is the same load-bearing seam the substrate review flags as G1: Pulse's heartbeat is not yet bound to issuance (epoch-bound minting, R1), and the batch-auction venue depends on a hardened epoch boundary to clear and settle against. Closing that seam is the precondition for the per-epoch market the whole serverless design rests on.



## 6. Role of PULSE (engine, settlement & issuance)

PULSE is the substrate; Markets of Truth is a *consumer* of it. Everything the game is at the protocol layer — a stake that meters a forecast, a CLAIM that lives on a content-addressed Web, a resolution that pays only on re-executed truth, a PLS reward that accrues to verified-useful curation rather than to luck — reduces to calls into one pure-Python, stdlib-only engine living under `/Users/develuse/repo/pulse/src/knitweb`. Markets of Truth is a knitweb: an L5 domain plugin that *imports* PULSE and never re-implements transport, gossip, the ledger, or issuance. This chapter sets out precisely what the engine settles, how the integer ledger conserves every stake and payout, how epoch-bound issuance rewards curation, how the Web replicates the market across peers, and what PULSE must still add to carry a regulator-credible information-curation market. The grounding is honest: most of what follows is ACTIVE and property-tested (the verified architecture reference records 1183/1184 suites green), and the issuance-to-heartbeat binding is the one load-bearing engine gap the market depends on.

### 6.1 The integer ledger: every stake and payout is a conservation-preserving Knit

A truth market is, mechanically, an accounting of who staked what against which CLAIM, and who is owed what when it resolves. PULSE already provides exactly this accounting as its L1 ledger, and Markets of Truth uses it unmodified. The discipline is non-negotiable and structural: **money and state are integers** (wei-style base units); no float ever touches a balance or a hash (`core/canonical.encode` rejects them at runtime); a stake is never a number in a database row but a dual-signed, content-addressed record.

| Primitive | Module | Role in Markets of Truth |
|---|---|---|
| `Knit` | `ledger/knit.py` | A single stake or payout: a dual-signed transfer over `[from, to, symbol, amount, from_nonce, timestamp, network]`. Signatures are excluded from `to_record()`; the `network` id is *inside* the signed bytes (EIP-155 anti-replay), so a stake signed for the PLS web can never be replayed elsewhere. |
| `Fiber` | `ledger/fiber.py` | An immutable, content-addressed snapshot of one account's state — one link in a trader's history. Fibers are never transferred; they are the audit trail of a position. |
| `Braid` | `ledger/braid.py` | A trader's append-only chain of Fibers; `braid.weave` enforces `seq+1`, prev-CID linkage, nonce monotonicity, and a spent-knit set so the same stake cannot be double-spent. |
| `AccountNode` | `ledger/node.py` | The trader's wallet: `propose`/`accept` runs the two-party staking handshake; `transfer_to` moves PLS. |
| `validate_knit` | `ledger/knitweb.py` | The settlement gate: positive-integer amount, distinct parties, dual signatures, nonce match, **no overdraft**, and **exact value conservation** (debit equals credit, to the unit). |

The economically decisive property is the last one. When a player or an agentic-LLM trader stakes PLS to curate a CLAIM or forecast an OUTCOME, `validate_knit` proves the debit on the staker's `Fiber` equals the credit on the market escrow's `Fiber` exactly — `blob.debit` raises on overdraft, conservation is checked to the integer. When the market resolves, the payout is another `Knit`, conservation-checked the same way. There is no "house" that can leak value, no rounding dust, no floating-point drift across a million micro-stakes. This is what lets the plan claim, truthfully, that Markets of Truth is *activity accounting* and not a gambling float: the ledger is a conservation law, and PLS is the integer quantity it conserves — never a speculative instrument, never a number that the operator can inflate.

One honest limitation carries forward (architecture gap G6): the receiver-side nonce does not increment (`knitweb.py:98`), so an account can be spammed with incoming stakes with no L1 rate limit. For a market that *invites* inbound stakes this matters; §6.5 names the flow-control work the market consumes from a hardened ledger.

### 6.2 Epoch-bound PLS issuance: rewarding verified curation, not gambling

PLS comes into existence in exactly one way, and that fact is the spine of the product's regulatory framing. `token/mint.py`'s `Treasury` has **no premine and no admin mint** (`mint.py:7`): the only path to native PLS is `Treasury.reward_verified_work`, which proves the work *first*. The flow is: gate on sampled re-execution (`pouw.job.verify`) — fraud mints nothing and is slashable; anti-replay on the proof digest, so a colluding consumer+worker cannot resubmit the same proof to mint unboundedly (`mint.py:142`); settle the consumer's escrow to the worker as a conservation-preserving `Knit`; then mint the **bounded** reward as a *coinbase* `Fiber` whose `knit` field is the issuance CID, so the Braid's spent-knit guard makes a given issuance un-replayable. The reward is `escrow * rate_num // rate_den`, clamped so it can never exceed the escrow consumed (`EmissionPolicy.reward`, `mint.py:68`): **mint ≤ proven economic demand.**

For Markets of Truth this is the mechanism that distinguishes the product from a betting parlour. PLS is not paid out for *winning a wager*; it is minted for *verified-useful curation work* — re-executing a claim's evidence, re-deriving a resolution from cited provenance, distilling a calibrated reliability score (Chapter 7's LENS). The forecast stake is settled by conservation (no issuance); the *new* PLS that enters the economy is demand-gated reward for work that peers re-checked. A trader who merely guesses an outcome wins a redistributed, conserved pot; a curator who produces verified-useful information is the only party who causes issuance. That asymmetry is designed, not incidental, and it is enforced in integer code today.

The honest open seam (architecture gap G1, the highest-leverage increment in the roadmap): the `Pulse` heartbeat exists and chains (`core/pulse.py`: integer-only `Beat(epoch, timestamp, state_root, prev_beat)`, monotonic `verify_chain()`), but `token.mint` currently caps emission by escrow plus an optional `max_supply` — **not** by epoch. The source comment is explicit: "binding a mint cap to a Beat/epoch is a future wiring, not yet implemented" (`pulse.py:7-11`). For a truth market this is the difference between "PLS is minted whenever a resolution clears" and "issuance has a heartbeat-governed schedule the whole market can reason about and audit per season." Closing it — carry an `epoch_mint_cap` on the `Beat`, gate `reward_verified_work` to per-epoch escrowed demand, settle mints atomically at the epoch boundary — is buildable entirely within `core/pulse.py` + `token/mint.py` + `pouw`, with **no p2p surface**, and it is the prerequisite for the `Beat → state-root` checkpoint that an anti-manipulation light-client needs (§6.5).

### 6.3 PoUW resolution: re-execution, quorum, dispute window

Market resolution is where oracle-soundness is won or lost, and PULSE supplies the whole apparatus as its L4 PoUW layer — the same machinery that secures any verified-work claim, repurposed to resolve OUTCOMES.

A resolution proposal is escrowed (`pouw/escrow.settle_on_verify` pays a worker only on verify, conservation-preserving). A committee is selected with the proposer **excluded** (`committee.select_committee`), the sample size `k` is computed (`sampling.required_samples`), each verifier independently re-executes and emits a `Verdict` (`verify.run_committee`), and `quorum.tally` aggregates to a Byzantine-fault-tolerant `⌊2n/3⌋+1` outcome (`quorum.py:86`). A resolution that no `2/3`+1 quorum can reproduce does not settle — the single most important anti-manipulation property a truth market can have.

Around that, the **dispute window** (`pouw/dispute.py`, `DisputeWindowLedger`) is the time-locked safety net the game surfaces to players as the canonical "challenge period." Its core invariant is enforced in the constructor: `release_delay > dispute_window` (`dispute.py:103`), so staked escrow can *never* release while a dispute could still land. Until `slashable_until = submit_beat + dispute_window`, any verifier can dispute; `dispute_by_quorum` slashes collateral only on a `DETECTED_FAULT` (a quorum of mismatches against a worker who claimed success, `dispute.py:215`); payout happens only at/after `release_beat = submit_beat + release_delay`, strictly after the window closes (`dispute.release`). Crucially these are **Beat-counted** windows — which is exactly why epoch-bound issuance (§6.2) and the dispute clock want to ride the same `Pulse`: a market whose challenge windows and whose mint cadence both derive from one auditable heartbeat is a market whose resolution policy vBank (Chapter 9) can govern coherently.

### 6.4 The Web: p2p replication of the market as a content-addressed graph

The market itself — every CLAIM, every OUTCOME definition, every evidence bundle, every reliability attestation — is not a server's database; it is the **Web**, PULSE's L3 content-addressed graph (`fabric/web.py`), replicated by the live `FabricNode` gossip peer (`fabric/node.py`). A CLAIM is a woven record; the links between a claim, its evidence, and its resolution are typed `Edge`s; the shared market state is the `web_state_root` (`fabric/items.py`) — one value that is byte-identical on every honest peer and is what the market anchors to OriginTrail.

Propagation preserves byte-identity end to end, so a CLAIM's CID never changes in flight. The path is lazy (`inv → getdata → inv-data`): a node announces a CID only, a peer requests just the CIDs it lacks, and the announcer serves the *verbatim* stored frame from its `_frames` cache. Target selection is the bounded gossipsub mesh; a reconnecting trader runs **Erlay** set-reconciliation (`p2p/reconcile.py`, `ReconcileSession`) to pull `O(diff)` not `O(total)`; the unconditional backstop is **anti-entropy** (`FabricNode.sync_from`). Every dial is ban-gated and per-peer byte/probe-budgeted on the single `BaseNode._dispatch` seam. Most decisively for an adversarial market, ingest is hardened against partition: `_ingest_signed` verifies the author signature and routes node-vs-edge **off the signed record itself** (`_is_edge_record`, `node.py`, issue #163), so a relayer cannot flip a CLAIM into a link and split the market's state root — a Sybil flood of validly-signed records is throttled by a per-peer ingest budget before it exhausts memory. Discovery is serverless (`p2p/kademlia.py`, a 634-line DHT, plus `p2p/discovery.py`), and `peer_identity_gate.py` + `reputation.py` keep a flood of fake traders from drowning an honest market — the p2p complement to the personhood anti-Sybil gate (Chapter 5).

### 6.5 The seam the market consumes, and what PULSE must add

Markets of Truth binds PULSE through three deliberately narrow facades, never its internals. **`gateway.App` / `serve(app)`** (`gateway.py`) is the turnkey layer: stable identity from an external id (`App.actor`), a persistent shared Web, a turnkey economy (`transfer`), BFT validation (`App.validate(verdicts)` → `pouw.quorum.tally`), and OriginTrail anchoring (`App.anchor`), all exposable over plain HTTP/JSON (`/actor`, `/transfer`, `/attest`, `/link`, `/validate`, `/web`) so any runtime drives the engine without a line of Python. **`sdk.Wallet`** is the friendly value-path facade (`create`, `balance`, `pay`, plus `distill_bundle`/`verify_bundle` for the LENS read path). **`FabricNode`** is the raw async peer for processes wanting live gossip. Agentic-LLM traders are first-class through exactly this seam: an agent is an `AccountNode` that `pay`s stakes and `weave`s evidence like any human peer.

To carry a credible, manipulation-resistant truth market, the engine — not the market plugin — must grow four capabilities, in priority order:

1. **Epoch-bound issuance (§6.2).** The single highest-leverage increment: bind `EmissionPolicy` to the `Beat` so issuance and the dispute clock share one auditable heartbeat. Solo lane, no p2p surface.
2. **A `Beat → state-root` checkpoint and light-proof path.** Expose `fabric/feed.py`'s feed/multiproof surface as a verifiable light read so a thin client (or an oracle auditor) can confirm a CLAIM or a resolution against `web_state_root` without full replication — the basis of a publicly checkable, non-repudiable market state.
3. **Receiver-side flow control (gap G6).** A market invites inbound stakes; the ledger needs an L1 rate limit so an account cannot be griefed with junk incoming transfers.
4. **A hardened, typed canonical boundary (gaps G3/G4).** Per-container length budgets in `canonical._decode` (today only `MAX_DEPTH`/`MAX_ITEMS`) to kill heap-amplification from untrusted peers, and an integer-only relation-weight type closing the float quarantine in `interpret`, so a reliability score can never trip a determinism cliff mid-resolution.

The strategic point is that none of this is the market's to build. The market's job is CLAIMS, forecasts, resolution policy, and play; PULSE's job is to be the engine that makes that play a real, conservation-sound, demand-gated crypto Web. Three of the four additions extend abstractions that already exist and are tested. That is what makes the plan credible: the settlement substrate is built and import-stable; the remaining engine work is rigor — heartbeat-governed issuance, light proofs, flow control, and a typed canonical wall — not a rewrite.



## 7. Role of LENS (the reliability oracle)

A truth/prediction market lives or dies on one question: *who decides what is true, and can that decider be bought, fooled, or flooded?* The history of the category is a history of oracle failure. In a single 2025 UMA dispute, a large token holder cast roughly 25% of the votes across three accounts and forced a $7 million payout on a demonstrably false resolution, having held the matching market position the whole time; in January 2026 a trader manipulated a 15-minute XRP market on Polymarket by buying "UP" shares and then spending $1 million on Binance to briefly pump the spot price into a $233,000 profit ([orochi.network](https://orochi.network/blog/oracle-manipulation-in-polymarket-2025), [webopedia](https://www.webopedia.com/crypto/learn/polymarkets-uma-oracle-controversy/)). The structural lesson the industry is now drawing — that "purely on-chain, token-weighted voting is insufficient for complex real-world disputes and must be augmented with reputation-based and evidence-based mechanisms" ([crypto-economy](https://crypto-economy.com/the-bottleneck-for-prediction-markets-its-all-about-resolution-infrastructure/)) — is exactly the design space LENS occupies in Markets of Truth.

LENS (the `knitweb.interpret` lobe, `retrieve` → `distill` → `quantize` → `feedback`) is the substrate's **reliability oracle**: a calibrated-confidence, abstention-capable, provenance-citing scorer of CLAIMS over the evidence Web. The single most important architectural commitment of this chapter is what LENS is *not*. **LENS does not resolve markets.** It advises. Resolution authority is split across PoUW re-execution, the dispute window, personhood, and vBank policy — never concentrated in one model, one scorer, or one quorum. LENS produces a *signed, replayable, provenance-cited reliability artifact*; PoUW + dispute + personhood *decide* whether that artifact is admitted to the canonical record and at what weight. This separation is what makes the oracle attackable only through the union of four independent trust boundaries rather than through any one of them.

### 7.1 What LENS actually computes (lens #5–8 lineage)

The lens #1–4 lineage shipped deterministic retrieval and gated distillation; lens #5–8 is the reliability layer that turns a *relation selection* into a *calibrated reliability score*. The pipeline is deliberately small and pure-Python so two independent spiders converge on byte-identical output.

**Retrieve (`retrieve.py`, `StageTag.SETTLEMENT`).** A claim under evaluation seeds a deterministic, subscription-gated graph walk over the *converged* evidence Web — `Web.traverse` / `neighbors` from query-derived seeds, optionally unioned with a `SpatialIndex.near` hit set, with `provenance.ancestry` attached for ranking. Every candidate is re-validated against the durable Web (`cid in web.nodes`) *after* any backend planner runs, so a retrieval backend "must never introduce cids that are not in the woven source of truth." Candidates are ranked by an integer reputation read off edge metadata (`_candidate_reputation`), giving a stable `CandidateSet` keyed to a `web_state_cid` — if the snapshot has crossed a Web epoch, the query *fails fast* rather than scoring stale evidence (`web_state_cid mismatch`). This is the anti-manipulation foundation: a claim is only ever scored against attested, epoch-pinned, content-addressed evidence that any peer can re-pull by hash.

**Distill (`distill.py`).** A strictly bounded loop (`max_iters`, `max_prompt_bytes`) selects relations from the frontier *without ever concatenating evidence into a single model prompt* — the module docstring is explicit that it "consumes `CandidateSet` without concatenating relation content into a single prompt." Every emitted relation passes `_gate_relation`: subject, predicate, and object CIDs must each be present in `web.nodes`, must each be `attest.node_is_attested`, and must each have acyclic provenance (`provenance.is_acyclic`). The output is a signed `Selection` carrying `relation_sources` (the candidate union behind each relation) and `intermediate_cids` — a full, replayable audit trail. This is the **anti-hallucination gate** (see 7.4): a relation an agent simply *invented* references CIDs that are not woven and attested, so `gate_relations` drops it before it can reach the record.

**Quantize (`quantize.py`).** Reliability is an *integer*. `quantize_weight` collapses `reputation` (integer), `recency`, and `pouw_score` into a deterministic, bounded `0..max_weight` weight via fixed-point arithmetic — `(6000·rep + 600·recency_milli + 7·pouw_milli) // 10000`, with `int(x*1000)` as the *single* float touch at the edge "so the blend itself is pure integer arithmetic." This matters beyond hygiene: a float leaking onto a canonical path would raise mid-distill (Gap **G3**), and more deeply, an integer-only reliability weight is *cross-machine deterministic*, so two honest scorers produce the identical signed bundle and the identical CID. A reliability score that cannot be reproduced bit-for-bit is a score that cannot be disputed; determinism is the precondition for accountability.

**Feedback (`feedback.py`, `StageTag.SETTLEMENT`).** Reliability is *replayable and revisable*. Each market outcome or curation confirmation is a signed `FeedbackEvent` (`confirms-relation` edge, secp256k1, `outcome ∈ {upheld, overturned}`) woven into the Web. `replay_feedback` deterministically folds these onto a relation slice — `+1` upheld, `−1` overturned, floored at `max(0, weight + Σδ)` — so a claim's reliability is the *integer sum of verified outcomes over time*, not a model's momentary opinion. This is the calibration loop: LENS's confidence in a class of claims is continuously corrected against what PoUW and the dispute window actually settled.

| LENS stage | Module | Output | Trust property |
|---|---|---|---|
| Retrieve | `retrieve.py` | `CandidateSet` (epoch-pinned, scope-gated) | Evidence is attested & re-pullable by hash |
| Distill | `distill.py` | signed `Selection` + provenance trail | No fabricated relations admitted |
| Quantize | `quantize.py` | integer reliability weight `0..255` | Cross-machine deterministic, disputable |
| Feedback | `feedback.py` | signed `FeedbackEvent`, replayed deltas | Calibrated against settled outcomes |

### 7.2 Calibrated confidence and abstention

The reliability layer treats *abstention as a first-class output*, not a failure. The 2025 literature is now unambiguous that standard accuracy objectives "reward confident guessing over calibrated uncertainty" — a model that says "I don't know" on 20% of questions and is right 95% on the rest scores *worse* on naive accuracy than one that guesses everything and hits 80% ([Know Your Limits, TACL 2025](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/393331033_Know_Your_Limits_A_Survey_of_Abstention_in_Large_Language_Models)). For a market oracle this incentive is catastrophic: a scorer rewarded for never abstaining will confidently price claims it cannot ground. LENS inverts the incentive. When the retrieved evidence frontier is thin, contradictory, or provenance-broken, distillation emits *fewer or zero gated relations* — a structurally low or null reliability weight — and the market reads that as **"insufficient evidence to price,"** which routes the claim to longer dispute windows and higher curation bounties rather than to a confident-but-wrong number. Conformal-prediction and selective-prediction techniques ([I-CALM](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.03904)) supply the statistical backing for *when* an agentic distiller should abstain; the substrate makes abstention *cheaper than fabrication* by paying PoUW reward only for relations that survive re-execution. Crucially, the codebase's own jury-theorem caveat holds: confidence-calibrated agents only improve collective accuracy when their errors are *independent* ([Epistemic Filtering, arXiv 2602.22413](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.22413)) — which is why LENS scores are an input to a diverse PoUW committee, never a substitute for one.

### 7.3 How LENS feeds pricing and resolution without becoming the oracle

LENS reliability weights enter the market at two seams, both *advisory*:

1. **Pricing prior.** A claim's standing LENS weight (the replayed integer from `feedback.py`) is published to MONITOR and consumed by traders and agentic forecasters as a *prior*, not a settlement. It shapes liquidity and odds; it never moves PLS on its own.
2. **Resolution input.** At resolution, the distilled `Selection` re-enters the PoUW flow as a **SPLIT-verified job** (`split_settles`), which the architecture state confirms "requires deterministic re-check AND a closed dispute window AND no upheld dispute" before settlement. The committee independently re-executes the distillation (`verify.run_committee`), `quorum.tally` aggregates to the BFT `⌊2n/3⌋+1` threshold, and only *then* does escrow release and PLS mint.

The separation of powers is therefore concrete and enforced in code, not promised in prose:

| Concern | Owner | LENS role |
|---|---|---|
| What evidence exists | LENS retrieve/distill | **produces** (gated, signed) |
| Is the score reproducible | PoUW re-execution (`verify`/`quorum`) | re-checked, can be **rejected** |
| Was it contested | `pouw.dispute` window + slash | LENS output **quarantined** if upheld |
| Who may score/vote | personhood gate (one-person-one-scope) | gated peer like any other |
| Fee/resolution policy | vBank | LENS weight is **policy input** |

Had this split governed the UMA case, the conflicted whale's stake would have been one personhood-gated vote inside a re-executed, dispute-windowed quorum — not 25% of a token-weighted tally. The CFTC's February 2026 amicus filings, arguing event contracts are CEA derivatives while five states press a gambling theory ([chainalysis](https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/crypto-prediction-markets/)), make the framing non-negotiable: Markets of Truth is **decentralized information-curation and verified-useful-work**, and LENS is an evidence-reliability instrument. PLS is activity accounting — minted only for curation/scoring work that survives re-execution — never a wager on the score.

### 7.4 The anti-hallucination gate: agents cannot poison the record

The defining risk of an *agentic* market is record poisoning: an autonomous LLM trader fabricating evidence to move a price. LENS's gate makes this structurally hard. A relation is admissible only if (a) all three CIDs are woven into the durable Web, (b) all three are `attest.node_is_attested`, (c) provenance is acyclic, and (d) it survives committee re-execution under a closed dispute window — and even then its reliability weight is the *integer replay of settled outcomes*, so a single fabrication carries near-zero standing weight until independently confirmed. An agent that hallucinates a citation produces a relation referencing a non-existent CID, which `_gate_relation` drops silently ("fabricated nodes are never emitted"). An agent that fabricates *and* weaves a fake evidence node still faces re-execution, the dispute window, slashing of escrowed PLS on upheld fault, and a personhood gate that bounds how many identities it can muster. LENS does not ask agents to be honest; it makes verified honesty the only economically rational strategy, and routes everything else to abstention and slash. That is the reliability oracle's real contract: it advises loudly, decides nothing alone, and leaves an immutable, re-pullable, provenance-cited trail behind every number it publishes.



## 8. Role of MONITOR (market integrity & observability)

A prediction or curation market lives or dies on one question its participants ask constantly: *can I trust what I am looking at?* On a centralised event-contract venue that question is answered by an operator — an exchange publishes a price, a volume figure, a resolution, and the user trusts the operator's database and its compliance department. Markets of Truth has no such operator. There is no central exchange to audit, no status page to believe, no admin who can be subpoenaed into honesty. The integrity of the market is instead a property of the Pulse substrate: every stake is a dual-signed Knit, every claim is a content-addressed record, every resolution is a `⌊2n/3⌋+1` committee verdict over a closed dispute window. **MONITOR is the instrument that makes that substrate-level integrity *legible* — and, critically, *locally verifiable* — without anyone having to trust MONITOR itself.** This chapter describes what the existing `knitweb_monitor` dashboard already is, what a truth-market demands beyond it, and the specific observability surfaces — order-flow maps, reputation views, dispute timelines, anomaly detection — that the funded work must add.

### 8.1 What MONITOR is today

`knitweb-monitor` (`/Users/develuse/repo/knitweb-monitor`) is a **zero-dependency, stdlib-only, cross-platform** dashboard — a single ~380-line module (`src/knitweb_monitor/__init__.py`) that runs identically on Windows, macOS and Linux from a `ThreadingHTTPServer` on `127.0.0.1:8990`. Its design discipline is exactly right for a trust instrument and must be preserved: it is **read-only**. As the README states, "it polls HTTP endpoints and reads wallet snapshots — it never writes state, moves funds, or speaks a node's wire protocol." A monitor that cannot mutate the market cannot itself become an attack surface or a point of privileged control; this is a precondition for it being a *trust* tool rather than another operator to trust.

Today it presents three tabs, driven by a handful of JSON endpoints (`/api/nodes`, `/api/graph`, `/api/molgang`, `/api/health`):

| Surface | What it reads | Truth-market analogue |
|---|---|---|
| **Nodes** | per-node PLS balance, address, `seq`/`nonce`, a live transfer feed, port liveness (`read_nodes`) | per-participant stake balances, settlement feed, node health |
| **Knowledge graph** | the woven fabric as a force-directed graph: ledger Knits (blue) + the domain knowledge layer `subject —relation→ object`, ⚓ for OriginTrail-anchored (`build_graph`, `_game_links`) | the curated-claim graph: CLAIMS, their relations, and which carry external anchors |
| **MOLGANG / sessions** | each live session's tables, seated players, woven fabric and confirmation counts (`read_molgang`) | each open MARKET: participants, open positions, resolution state |

Two implementation choices matter for the truth-market port. First, the graph layout is a **deterministic** Fruchterman–Reingold (`_layout`, fixed `seed=7`) when `networkx` is absent — meaning two independent observers compute the *same* picture from the same fabric, a small but real step toward reproducible evidence. Second, MONITOR reads ledger state through the optional `knitweb` package (`_load_knitweb` → `store.load_node`), walking each node's `braid.fibers` and recomputing balances from the immutable Fiber chain rather than trusting a reported number. That "recompute, don't believe" posture is the seed of everything Chapter 8 must grow.

### 8.2 Why a truth-market needs more than a liveness viewer

A liveness viewer answers "is the node up and what is its balance." A truth-market integrity console must answer a harder set of questions, because the failure modes of a prediction/curation market are *adversarial and economic*, not merely operational. The architecture review rates `pouw.dispute/collateral/escrow` at maturity 5 (the `release_delay > dispute_window` safety invariant is enforced in the constructor, `dispute.py:97-102`), and `quorum`/`committee`/`sampling` are production-grade — so the integrity *mechanisms* exist on-substrate. What is missing is their **observability**: a market participant, a regulator, or a journalist must be able to *see* that a resolution was committee-verified, that a dispute window was honoured, that no single cluster of identities curated a claim into existence, and that liquidity is real rather than wash-traded. MONITOR is where those substrate guarantees become visible facts. The four new surfaces below are the funded deliverable.

### 8.3 Surface one — live market health: liquidity, volume, open markets

The first promotion is from "sessions" to **markets**. For each open market MONITOR must show, refreshed on the existing 2–3s cadence: total PLS staked (open interest), the curate-side vs. forecast-side split, recent settlement volume reconstructed from the Knit feed, the number of distinct *personhood-gated* participants (not raw accounts — see §8.5), time-to-resolution, and the current resolution/dispute phase. Crucially these are not numbers the market *asserts*; they are **recomputed from primitives** the way `read_nodes` already recomputes balances from the Braid. Open interest is the sum of escrowed collateral in `DisputeWindowLedger`; volume is the sum of conservation-preserving settlement Knits; the participant count is the count of distinct personhood nullifier scopes. A market-health view built this way cannot be spoofed by a misreporting front-end, because MONITOR derives it from the same signed records every peer holds. A new `/api/markets` endpoint, structurally parallel to `read_molgang`, exposes per-market `{open_interest, volume_window, n_personhood, phase, resolve_at}` for the dashboard and for export.

### 8.4 Surface two — order-flow maps

A central exchange audits order flow by reading its own matching engine; Markets of Truth has no matching engine, so order flow *is* the Knit graph. MONITOR already renders ledger Knits as a blue layer in `build_graph`; the truth-market version makes that an **order-flow map** — a directed, time-windowed graph of who staked toward which side of which claim, edge-weighted by PLS and coloured by side (curate-verify / curate-refute / forecast-yes / forecast-no). This is the single most powerful manipulation-detection surface available, precisely *because* the venue is trustless: on a P2P market every stake is a public, signed, content-addressed record, so the complete order book is observable to everyone, forever — something no centralised order book offers. The map must support: temporal scrubbing (replay how a market's flow built up to a suspicious resolution); cluster highlighting (rings of accounts that consistently co-stake); and a "follow the collateral" trace from initial escrow through `escrow.settle_on_verify` to the coinbase mint in `Treasury.reward_verified_work`. Because the layout is deterministic, an order-flow map MONITOR draws is reproducible by any third party from the same fabric snapshot — evidence, not opinion.

### 8.5 Surface three — reputation and personhood

LENS (Chapter 6) produces calibrated, abstaining reliability scores for claims and curators; personhood (the cross-cutting gate, `personhood.gate.require_personhood`) enforces one-person-one-scope via a nullifier with epoch-pinned non-revocation. MONITOR is where both become a public reputation surface. For every curator and every agentic-LLM trader, the dashboard shows: LENS reliability (calibration, Brier-style accuracy on resolved claims, abstention rate), total verified-useful curation, dispute record (raised / upheld / overturned), and — visibly distinct — **whether the identity is a single personhood scope or an unverified account whose stake does not count toward quorum**. This last distinction is the anti-Sybil firewall made legible: a market whose "consensus" rests on accounts that never passed the personhood gate is shown as exactly that. Reputation here is non-transferable and earned, so MONITOR can expose it as a soulbound attribute of an address without creating a tradeable, gameable score. A reputation view turns the abstract claim "Sybil-resistant" into a number a sceptic can check.

### 8.6 Surface four — dispute timelines and resolution audit

Resolution is the moment of maximum adversarial pressure, and the substrate already handles it carefully: a split-settled job requires deterministic re-check **and** a closed dispute window **and** no upheld dispute (`job.py:332-351`); `DisputeWindowLedger.submit` opens the window with staked collateral, `dispute_by_quorum` slashes only on `DETECTED_FAULT`, and `release` pays strictly after `release_beat`. MONITOR must render this as a **dispute timeline** per market: when the claim resolved, the beat at which the dispute window opened and (provably) closed, every dispute filed with its collateral and quorum verdict, every slash, and the final release. Because release timing is epoch-bound to the Pulse heartbeat (R1), the timeline is anchored to verifiable beats, not wall-clock claims. The audit view lets anyone confirm the safety invariant held — that no payout occurred before the window closed — which is the property a regulator or counterparty most needs to see. A new `/api/disputes` endpoint surfaces `{market, resolved_beat, window_open, window_close, disputes[], slashes[], released}` straight from the dispute ledger.

### 8.7 Anomaly and manipulation detection

On top of these surfaces MONITOR runs a small, **deterministic, explainable** anomaly layer — heuristics, not a black-box model, so every flag is reproducible and citable. The initial detectors: *wash-trading* (collateral cycling between a tight account cluster with no net informational contribution), *late-flood staking* (a sudden one-sided surge near resolution, the classic oracle-capture pattern), *Sybil-curation* (a claim woven into existence by accounts sharing no personhood scope), *quorum anomaly* (a verdict that barely cleared `⌊2n/3⌋+1` with correlated voters), and *LENS divergence* (a resolution contradicting LENS's calibrated confidence, which should *abstain*-or-flag rather than over-rule). Each detector emits a signed, reproducible evidence bundle pointing at the exact CIDs and Knits that triggered it. MONITOR does not adjudicate — it has no write path — it *surfaces*; adjudication remains with the dispute mechanism and, ultimately, VBANK policy (Chapter 7).

### 8.8 Trustless transparency: trust without trusting MONITOR

The defining requirement, and the reason MONITOR is a chapter rather than a footnote, is that it must manufacture trust **without becoming something to trust**. Three properties achieve this. First, **read-only by construction** — it never writes the fabric, so it cannot corrupt the market it observes. Second, **recompute-don't-believe** — every figure is derived from signed, content-addressed primitives (Knits, Fibers, dispute-ledger entries, personhood nullifiers), exactly as `read_nodes` already recomputes balances from the Braid, so a lying front-end is detectable by re-derivation. Third, **reproducible evidence** — deterministic layout and deterministic detectors mean any party can run their own MONITOR against the same fabric and get byte-identical conclusions; MONITOR exports local state roots and evidence bundles a third party can independently verify. The honest framing of the whole product depends on this: Markets of Truth is decentralised information-curation with activity-accounting PLS, and MONITOR is the surface that lets *anyone* — participant, auditor, regulator — confirm that claim against the records themselves, rather than taking the project's word for it. That is the difference between a status page and a trust instrument, and it is the difference between a market you must trust and a market you can verify.



## 9. Role of VBANK (governance, fees & treasury)

A truth market is only as trustworthy as the rules that govern *who may create a market, how it resolves, what a dispute costs, and where the fees go* — and in a serverless system there is no operator to set those rules from a control panel. **VBANK** is Markets of Truth's constitutional layer: the L5 `knitwebs/vbank` governance plugin (`poll/ranked/liquid/tally`, ACTIVE, end-to-end tested in `test_vbank_end_to_end.py` and `test_vbank_liquid.py`) bound to the `token/mint.py` **Treasury** and the `crowdfunding` settlement session. It is the mechanism by which a community of human players and agentic-LLM traders — not a company — decides the market venue's policy, and by which those decisions become deterministic, content-addressed, personhood-gated, and auditable records on the Web rather than a multisig's discretion. This chapter describes what VBANK governs, the voting machinery it uses, how it controls fees and the epoch-bound treasury, and why a deterministic on-fabric constitution is the only honest way to run a curation venue with no server.

### 9.1 What VBANK governs

VBANK is the policy authority over five surfaces that, in any centralized prediction venue, would be set unilaterally by the operator. In Markets of Truth they are governed parameters, each a signed VBANK proposal that tallies to a deterministic verdict and writes a new policy record:

| Governance surface | Concretely controls | Why it must be community-held |
|---|---|---|
| **Market creation / whitelisting** | Who may instantiate a CLAIM market; which claim *templates* (binary, scalar, categorical) and topic domains are admissible; minimum curation stake | Permissionless creation invites spam and bad-faith "claims"; an operator whitelist re-centralizes. VBANK is the middle path. |
| **Fee policy** | The curation fee, resolution fee, and dispute-bond schedule (all integer-rational); the split between treasury, verifiers, and the Lens reliability pool | Fees are the venue's monetary policy; whoever sets them controls who profits from curation |
| **Resolution-source policy** | Which resolution sources are admissible per domain (PoUW re-execution, attested oracle bundles, Lens-scored evidence); the resolution-source *priority order* | "How a market resolves" is the single most manipulable surface in any truth market; it cannot be an operator's discretion |
| **Dispute-escalation policy** | The `dispute_window`/`release_delay` defaults, the collateral `Margin`, the BFT verifier `threshold`, and the escalation ladder (single dispute → committee → community vote) | The cost and procedure of contesting a resolution is the venue's due-process; it must be constitutional, not ad-hoc |
| **Treasury (epoch-bound issuance)** | What counts as verified-useful curation; per-epoch emission caps; treasury spend on public goods (seed claims, Lens calibration, MONITOR infrastructure); sanctioning of misbehaving agents | PLS is an activity-accounting unit; its emission and the venue's commons must be governed, not minted at will |

The design discipline is the same one the molgang plan applies to chemistry: **VBANK re-implements nothing.** It is a thin L5 plugin that imports the engine's voting primitives, the personhood gate, the quorum/dispute machinery, and the Treasury, and composes them into a market-venue constitution. Every governance act is a real protocol operation — a signed proposal record, a personhood-gated ballot, a deterministic `tally` — never a re-skinned admin call.

### 9.2 The voting machinery: poll, ranked, liquid, tally

VBANK ships three voting modes plus a deterministic tally, each suited to a different class of governance question, and each personhood-gated so that one human (or one credentialed agent) carries one base voice — not one voice per wallet.

**Poll voting** handles binary and small-categorical constitutional questions: *admit this resolution source?*, *raise the dispute bond to X?* A poll opens a proposal record, accepts personhood-gated ballots within an epoch window, and tallies to an integer verdict. It is the workhorse for fee-schedule and policy toggles.

**Ranked voting** handles choose-one-of-many questions where the field matters — selecting among competing resolution-source orderings, or among parameter bundles for a new market template. Ranked ballots elect an option under a deterministic, integer-only count, avoiding the vote-splitting failure that plagues plurality choices over crowded policy fields.

**Liquid (delegated) voting** is the load-bearing mechanism for a venue whose voters are mostly *not* domain experts. A player who knows nothing about, say, epidemiological claim resolution can delegate their voice on that topic to a high-reliability curator, while voting directly on fee policy they understand. Delegation is transitive and revocable, and — critically for Markets of Truth — it can be **scoped to a topic**, so reliability earned in the Lens scoring layer flows into governance weight *only where it was earned*. This directly answers the epistemic-delegation problem now active in the DAO-governance literature (arXiv:2505.04136): liquid democracy lets a small set of competent voters carry decisions they understand without concentrating control over everything.

**Tally** is the deterministic settlement of any vote into a verdict record. Like the PoUW `quorum.tally` it mirrors (`⌊2n/3⌋+1` BFT supermajority, outcomes `CONFIRMED`/`DETECTED_FAULT`/`DECLARED_FAULT`/`INCONCLUSIVE`), VBANK's tally is integer-only and reproducible: any peer holding the same ballot set recomputes the identical outcome, so the verdict is auditable and equivocation-free. There is no privileged counter. The result is a content-addressed policy record the whole Web converges on.

**Personhood-gating is the anti-plutocracy seam.** The hard problem of token-weighted governance is well documented: when a few holders own most of the token, "decentralization becomes nominal" and majority ownership becomes unilateral control (blockchain-council; arXiv:2505.00888). VBANK does *not* run on raw PLS weight. Every ballot passes `personhood.gate.require_personhood` — the one-person-one-scope nullifier, epoch-pinned non-revocation gate (`gate.py`) — so a base voice is bound to a unique, Sybil-resistant identity, not to a balance. Stake and reliability can *modulate* weight (a curator who has staked and been scored well by Lens carries more voice on their topics), but they cannot manufacture *new identities*. This is the structural advantage Chapter 2 names for ranking integrity, reused as a governance-integrity advantage: a serverless truth venue can run a one-person-one-vote constitution that token-weighted DAOs structurally cannot.

### 9.3 Governing resolution and dispute — the manipulation-resistance core

Because resolution policy is the most attackable surface of any truth market, VBANK's governance of it is first-class and tightly coupled to the PoUW dispute machinery from Chapter 8. VBANK does not *resolve* individual markets — that is the job of PoUW re-execution, the verifier committee, and the Lens oracle. VBANK sets the **rules under which** resolution happens, and owns the **escalation ladder** when a resolution is contested.

The ladder is built directly on `pouw/dispute.py` and `pouw/quorum.py`:

1. **Resolution submitted with collateral.** A market resolver submits an outcome to the `DisputeWindowLedger.submit` with staked collateral alongside the curation escrow. The safety invariant `release_delay > dispute_window` (enforced in the constructor, `dispute.py:103`) guarantees no resolver can be paid and exit before a dispute could still land — the "withdraw-before-dispute" attack is closed by construction.
2. **Single dispute.** Any peer may file a detected-mismatch `dispute` within `slashable_until = submit_beat + dispute_window`. A successful dispute *slashes* the resolver's collateral and *refunds* the curation escrow — fraud is never net-profitable, which the `collateral.py` sizing rule (`collateral ≥ payout_at_risk × margin`) makes exact.
3. **Committee escalation.** A contested resolution escalates to `dispute_by_quorum`: a `select_committee`-drawn jury (worker excluded, VRF-style unpredictable-yet-verifiable, `committee.py`) re-executes sampled evidence and emits `Verdict`s that `tally` aggregates. A slash lands *only* on `DETECTED_FAULT` — a BFT quorum of mismatches against a resolver that claimed success. An honest `DECLARED_FAULT` self-report refunds without slashing (`refund_declared_fault`). No lone verifier can slash honest work.
4. **Community escalation.** Where evidence is genuinely contested (a subjective or under-specified claim), the verdict escalates to a VBANK ranked/liquid vote — the venue's supreme court — which can also *retroactively amend the claim template* so the ambiguity cannot recur.

VBANK governs every dial in that ladder — the window lengths, the collateral margin, the BFT threshold, and the `apply_relevance_penalty` reputation hit (`dispute.py:333`) that feeds back into Lens reliability scoring. This is exactly the architecture the prediction-market-governance literature converges on: a market-resolution DAO whose core function is "adjudicating on-chain events, resolving oracle disputes, and validating outcomes" via a voting mechanism triggered when a reported outcome is challenged (chainscorelabs; the Augur/Gnosis dispute-vote pattern). Markets of Truth implements that pattern natively, with slashing economics that make a frivolous challenge or a fraudulent resolution *cost more than it can gain*.

### 9.4 The treasury and epoch-bound fee policy

VBANK is also the steward of the venue's commons. The treasury is the `token/mint.py` **Treasury** — and its single most important property is honesty about issuance: **there is no raw, ungated mint** (`mint.py:104`). Native PLS exists only as a demand-gated reward for verified-useful curation through `reward_verified_work`, which gates on PoUW verification, anti-replays each proof digest (no double-mint), settles the curation escrow conservation-preservingly, then mints a *bounded* reward (`reward ≤ escrow`, capped by `max_supply`). VBANK cannot vote new PLS into existence for itself; it can only govern the *policy parameters* of an emission that is already provably tied to verified work.

The R1 epoch-binding work (Chapter 6; PULSE_ARCHITECTURE_STATE G1/R1) is what turns this from accounting into monetary policy. Today the Treasury bounds emission by escrow and an optional `max_supply` but **not by epoch**. R1 carries an `epoch_mint_cap` on the Pulse Beat and gates `reward_verified_work` to per-epoch escrowed demand. Once that seam closes, VBANK governs a real, periodic budget: each epoch the venue mints at most a community-set cap, and VBANK's `token.economy` policy decides how the resulting treasury is spent — seeding high-value claims, funding Lens calibration runs, underwriting MONITOR infrastructure, and topping up the verifier reward pool. Fee policy is the inflow side of the same ledger: curation fees, resolution fees, and dispute bonds (all integer-rational) split by VBANK vote between treasury, verifiers, and the Lens reliability pool. Crowdfunding (`crowdfunding/{campaign,settlement}`, ACTIVE) lets the community pre-commit pledges to *create* a market or fund a public-good claim, settling only if the funding quorum is met — a personhood-gated, all-or-nothing campaign that never custodies funds on a trusted server.

### 9.5 Why a deterministic on-fabric constitution

The reason VBANK matters to the product thesis is that a serverless truth venue has nowhere else to put its rules. There is no admin to whitelist a market, no operator to set a fee, no support desk to overturn a bad resolution. Those decisions must live *as records on the Web the venue runs on*, produced by a process every peer can independently re-run and verify. VBANK delivers exactly that: personhood-gated ballots, deterministic integer tallies, content-addressed policy records, and a treasury that cannot mint outside verified work. The honest framing the whole plan maintains — **PLS is an activity-accounting unit, not a speculative instrument** — is enforced here at the constitutional layer: governance weight derives from unique personhood and earned reliability, not from holdings; emission is demand-gated and epoch-capped; and the venue's commons is spent by transparent vote. A community self-governs its market venue not because it is asked to trust VBANK, but because it never has to: it can recompute every verdict itself.

## 10. Agentic LLM Traders & the Agent Economy

### 10.1 The shift already happened — and why Markets of Truth is built for it

By mid-2026 the defining fact about open prediction markets is not that humans use them, but that machines do. On Polymarket, AI agents now account for over 30% of wallet activity, and 14 of the 20 most-profitable wallets are bots; agents turn a positive P&L at roughly 3× the human rate (37% of agent wallets vs. a 7–13% human baseline), and purpose-built traders such as Polystrat executed 4,200+ trades in their first live month with single-trade returns peaking at 376% ([CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/03/15/ai-agents-are-quietly-rewriting-prediction-market-trading); [New York City Servers](https://newyorkcityservers.com/blog/ai-agents-prediction-market-trading)). The reference architecture has converged: an LLM "brain" (typically Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.4, or a fine-tuned open model) reads market context, calls tools (`get_market_data`, `get_news`, `place_order`, `check_position`), and an exchange client signs the order ([Turbine](https://www.turbinefi.com/blog/how-to-compete-with-ai-agents-prediction-markets-2026)).

Markets of Truth treats this not as a threat to design around but as the **native participant class**. The structural claim of the broader knitweb thesis — that an autonomous agent which earns, reasons and acts on behalf of others *is, mechanically, a tiny economy* — is most visible here. An agent in a truth market needs four things at once: verifiable compute to justify its forecasts, durable provenance for the evidence it cites, a unique non-Sybil identity so it cannot vote with a thousand sock-puppets, and exact micro-settlement to be paid for useful curation. The 2026 mainstream assembles these from four unrelated systems glued by trust. Pulse provides them as facets of one content-addressed Web: the fabric is provenance, **PoUW** is verifiable compute, **personhood** is unique identity, the integer-only **Braid/Knit/Fiber** ledger is exact micro-settlement, and **PLS** ties activity to account. Crucially, this re-frames the agent's incentive. A Polymarket bot is rewarded for *being right about prices*; a Markets-of-Truth agent is rewarded for *verified-useful curation and forecasting* — work other spiders re-execute and uphold — which is why PLS is an activity-accounting unit and not a wagering chip.

### 10.2 Three agent roles, one engine

An agentic trader in Markets of Truth is not a chatbot bolted onto a UI. It knits the same canonical-CBOR records, signs with the same secp256k1 key, settles the same PLS Knit, passes the same personhood gate, and is held to the same verification quorum as a human player. Three first-class roles compose the agent side of the market, each binding to existing engine packages rather than new bespoke logic.

| Agent role | What it does | Engine seam it rides | Reward path |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Curation agent** | Proposes/verifies CLAIMS: extracts a claim, cites attested evidence nodes, distils a provenance-bound relation bundle | `interpret.retrieve` → `interpret.distill` → `pouw.job` (SPLIT) | PLS for an upheld, re-executable distill bundle |
| **Forecasting agent** | Stakes PLS on OUTCOMES of an open market; publishes a calibrated probability + cited rationale | `vbank` market position + LENS confidence score; settled at resolution | PLS proportional to verified accuracy, not raw P&L |
| **Market-maker agent** | Posts two-sided liquidity, narrows spreads, absorbs flow within stake limits | `vbank` quoting surface + `pouw.collateral`/`escrow` bonds | Activity-accounted PLS rebate for liquidity that MONITOR confirms as real |

The curation agent is where LENS does its sharpest work. Rather than letting a model both retrieve *and* assert — the failure mode of ordinary RAG — the agent's free generation is confined to an advisory `RetrieveBackend` planner that may *propose* which candidate CIDs and which `(subject, predicate, object)` triple to attempt. The proposal is then funnelled through `distill._gate_relation`: membership (every CID exists in `web.nodes`), acyclicity (`provenance.is_acyclic`), and attestation (`attest.node_is_attested`). A claim that cannot be grounded in attested, acyclic, woven nodes is **never emitted**. An agent physically cannot publish a forecast rationale citing evidence that was never woven. This is the difference between an agent that argues convincingly and one that argues *verifiably* — and it directly answers the documented weakness of trading LLMs that "incur losses despite uniformly high stated confidence" ([arXiv PolyBench](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.14199)).

The forecasting agent's score is equally pointed. PolyBench found only two of seven SOTA models achieved positive returns while all reported high confidence; the lesson is that *self-reported* confidence is worthless. In Markets of Truth a forecasting agent's reward is governed by LENS's **calibrated-confidence + abstention** layer (lens #5–8, in design): confidence is the integer `weight` derived from attestable reputation, recency and verified-work signals — a number two parties recompute and agree on — and an honest **abstention** is a valid, non-penalised answer. An agent that abstains when ungrounded loses nothing; an agent that forecasts loudly and wrongly decays its Lens reliability and forfeits stake at resolution.

### 10.3 Where inference runs — edge, Z.AI, and donated-GPU spiders via PoUW

A serverless P2P market cannot assume a hyperscaler behind every agent. Inference in Markets of Truth runs on a three-tier ladder, and the tier is itself recorded so verification cost matches stake at risk.

1. **Edge / local.** A player's own machine or a small fine-tuned open model runs the curation/forecast loop locally. This is the default for low-stake markets and keeps the dapp truly serverless: no inference operator to subpoena, throttle, or trust.
2. **Z.AI and hosted endpoints.** For heavier reasoning an agent calls an external endpoint (Z.AI, or a frontier API). The model's *output* enters the economy only as an advisory proposal — it never touches the value path directly — so a hosted model's non-determinism is quarantined behind the deterministic `retrieve → distill` gate.
3. **Donated-GPU spiders via PoUW.** The most knitweb-native tier: spiders contributing GPU run distillation as a **proof-of-useful-work job**. `pouw/job.py` registers distillation as SPLIT-verified; `execute_distill` returns a signed bundle plus a deterministic `manifest_digest`, and `verify_distill` re-runs the identical pipeline against the same hash-pinned Web and asserts the digest matches. A committee of *other* spiders independently re-derives the bundle; agreement proves the work useful and mints bounded PLS as a coinbase Fiber, now epoch-bound to the Pulse heartbeat.

This ladder is exactly the seam the wider industry is reaching for but solving with single trusted oracles. The honest engineering note is that **full cryptographic proof of an LLM forward pass is not yet practical at frontier scale**: 2025's landmark DeepProve-1 produced the first zkML proof of a *full GPT-2 inference* — the "hello-world" of verifiable generative AI — but proving times for large transformers remain minutes-to-hours, and zkML "only scale[s] to small- or medium-sized neural networks" ([Lagrange](https://lagrange.dev/blog/deepprove-1); [Extropy](https://academy.extropy.io/pages/articles/zkml-singularity.html)). TEEs (SGX/TDX attestation) carry ~2× overhead with thin GPU support ([arXiv VET](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.15892)). Markets of Truth therefore does **not** claim to verify the model's reasoning cryptographically. It verifies the *useful artefact* — the deterministic, replayable distill bundle the model merely advised — which any spider re-executes for free. The model is allowed to be a black box precisely because the gate that admits its output is not. zkML and TEE attestation slot in later as optional confidence tiers on the inference record, not as a load-bearing dependency.

### 10.4 The agent economy: earning, spending, and agent-to-agent settlement

Agents are economic actors with their own wallets. A curation agent *earns* PLS for upheld bundles; it *spends* PLS to stake on its own forecasts, to bond market-maker collateral, and — the closing loop — to **buy inference** from donated-GPU spiders. The arXiv "Agent Economy" line of work describes exactly this pattern as the emerging shape of on-chain agents: self-managed wallets, GPU providers registering capacity, agents bidding for compute with their own keys, smart-contract settlement of allocation and payment ([arXiv 2602.14219](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.14219v1)). Markets of Truth implements that economy without a chain or a smart-contract VM: a compute request and its fulfilment are two records, and payment is one `Knit`.

Agent-to-agent settlement is where Pulse's integer ledger is decisive. The mainstream agentic-payments stack of 2026 — Google's **AP2** (60+ partners incl. PayPal, Coinbase, Mastercard) and the HTTP-native **x402** micropayment protocol (35M transactions on Solana by March 2026) — establishes that machine-to-machine payment is now standard infrastructure ([Cobo AP2 guide](https://www.cobo.com/post/ap2-protocol-complete-guide-to-agent-payments-for-web3-developers-2026); [Eco x402](https://eco.com/support/en/articles/12328618-x402-protocol-explained-how-ai-agents-pay-onchain)). But every one of those rails settles a *speculative stablecoin balance* over someone else's chain. A PLS Knit settles **verified activity** between exactly two parties with no third-party block space: `ledger/knitweb.validate_knit` enforces positive-integer amounts, distinct parties, dual signatures, nonce match, no overdraft, and exact value conservation. A forecasting agent paying a GPU spider for a distillation, or a market-maker paying a curation agent for a high-weight claim bundle, is a single dual-signed transfer woven into both braids — auditable, replayable, and unforgeable. Where interoperability with the outside agent web matters, an x402/AP2 gateway can expose a PLS settlement endpoint over plain HTTP/JSON via the engine's `serve(app)` surface; the bridge is an adapter, never a dependency, and PLS never leaves the activity-accounting frame.

The accounting honesty here is non-negotiable. PLS minted to agents is demand-gated reward for verified-useful-work — never a premine, never an admin mint, anti-replayed by proof digest and capped per epoch by the Beat. An agent cannot conjure PLS by trading cleverly; it can only earn PLS for curation and liquidity that other spiders re-executed and upheld. This is what keeps Markets of Truth an **information-curation / verified-work** system rather than a gambling product, and it is the property the regulatory framing (Chapter on compliance) rests on.

### 10.5 Guardrails: personhood, reliability gate, rate and stake limits

An agent-first market is an agent-first attack surface. The 2026 literature is candid that generative AI now produces live video indistinguishable from reality and LLM swarms argue more convincingly than humans, so one-shot CAPTCHAs and naïve proof-of-personhood "provide limited protection against long-term, large-scale Sybil participation" ([Exmon Academy](https://academy.exmon.pro/proof-of-personhood-2026-crypto-vs-deepfakes-ai-agents); [CCN](https://www.ccn.com/education/crypto/proof-of-personhood-human-only-governance-ai-explained/)). Markets of Truth does not try to *exclude* agents — it admits them under four composed, first-class guardrails, each an existing engine subsystem.

| Guardrail | Mechanism | Engine module | Attack it blunts |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Personhood** | One-person/agent-one-scope nullifier, pairwise DIDs, revocable VC/ZK backend, no PII on-fabric | `personhood/gate.py` | Sybil swarms voting/staking as a thousand puppets |
| **Reliability gate** | LENS calibrated-confidence + abstention; low-Lens agents down-weighted at tally | `interpret` (lens #5–8) | Confident-but-wrong agents dominating curation |
| **Re-execution quorum + dispute** | BFT ⌊2n/3⌋+1 committee re-runs the work; `release_delay > dispute_window`; slash on upheld fault | `pouw/quorum`, `pouw/dispute` | Fabricated curation, collusive resolution |
| **Rate / stake limits** | Per-peer ingest budgets, collateral bonds, vBank market-creation/stake caps | `p2p` budgets, `pouw/collateral`, `vbank` | Flood/spam, under-collateralised manipulation |

Two design points deserve emphasis. First, **personhood is the anti-sybil anchor even for agents**: a personhood credential can be issued to a registered autonomous agent as a distinct, revocable, pairwise scope, so the market knows it is dealing with one accountable agent identity — not a sock-puppet farm — without demanding the agent reveal a human operator. This mirrors the emerging view that PoP networks can themselves serve as decentralized oracles by sampling unique witnesses ([arXiv 2008.05300](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2008.05300)). Second, **oracle soundness is a swarm property, not a single judge.** The industry is moving from single-oracle resolution toward AI swarms verifying real-world truth ([crypto.news ETHGlobal](https://crypto.news/ai-agents-privacy-and-prediction-markets-define-ethglobal-cannes-2026-finalists/)); Markets of Truth's resolution is exactly such a swarm — a re-execution quorum plus a dispute window plus Lens reliability weighting — so no individual agent, however capable, can single-handedly resolve a market in its own favour. Manipulation is made *economically unprofitable and physically unmintable*: a bundle that the committee cannot reproduce is rejected, an overturned forecast decays the agent's Lens weight, and an upheld fault slashes collateral.

The strategic conclusion is that Markets of Truth turns the most alarming property of 2026 prediction markets — that bots out-trade humans and may exploit adversarial inputs — into its foundation. Agents are welcomed as first-class participants precisely because every move they make is a real protocol operation passed through a Sybil gate, a reliability gate, a re-execution quorum, and an exact-settlement ledger. The market does not pay agents to be lucky; it pays them to do curation and forecasting work that other agents can independently verify. That is the agent economy this substrate was built to carry.



## 11. Resolution, oracles & anti-manipulation: how a market finds truth

Every prediction or curation market lives or dies on one question: **when the market closes, who decides what is true — and why should anyone trust them?** This is the question that has sunk most decentralized prediction markets. Augur shipped a token-weighted reporting oracle and watched it stall under the cost and latency of disputes; UMA's Optimistic Oracle works but routes finality through a token-vote escalation that whales can dominate; centralized event-contract venues simply *are* the oracle and ask you to trust them. **Markets of Truth refuses a trusted oracle entirely.** A claim resolves because its resolution is a *deterministic program* that any peer can re-execute and a Byzantine committee independently agrees on — with a bonded dispute window, a personhood-bounded electorate, and an advisory Lens reliability signal layered on top. This chapter specifies that machinery against the actual Pulse modules (`pouw.committee`, `pouw.sampling`, `pouw.quorum`, `pouw.dispute`), then runs the threat model: bribery, sybil, last-mover, oracle-capture and ambiguous-claim attacks, each with a concrete mitigation. This is the hard core that makes the product credible.

### 11.1 The resolution pipeline: re-execution, not reporting

The foundational design choice is that **a market never asks a human "what happened?" — it asks a program "does this evidence satisfy the resolution criterion?"** A CLAIM, at creation, must bind a `resolution_spec`: a deterministic predicate over a typed evidence set (a set of content-addressed records on the Web — oracle feeds, attested documents, prior verified claims), expressed in the same bounded, replayable form a `SynapticCompileJob` already uses in `interpret/distill`. Resolution is therefore not an opinion; it is a **PoUW job whose "useful work" is computing the outcome and whose verification is re-execution**.

The pipeline reuses the production-hardened PoUW stack verbatim, in five stages:

| Stage | Module | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Submit | `pouw.dispute.DisputeWindowLedger.submit` | A *resolver* re-executes the `resolution_spec` over the evidence set, commits the outcome digest, and submits at integer beat `submit_beat` with staked **collateral** alongside the market's escrowed resolution fee. |
| 2. Committee draw | `pouw.committee.select_committee` | A jury of `k` verifiers is drawn from the eligible pool by `sha256(seed‖counter)`, the resolver **excluded from its own jury**. The `seed` derives from the committed outcome root plus a per-epoch Pulse beacon, so it cannot exist at commit time. |
| 3. Sampled re-check | `pouw.sampling.required_samples` | Each verifier re-executes a sample of the resolution computation sized so the hypergeometric *miss* probability of a corrupt outcome falls at or below target (exact `fractions.Fraction`, no floats). |
| 4. Quorum | `pouw.quorum.tally` | Verdicts aggregate to the BFT supermajority `k = ⌊2n/3⌋+1`, tolerating `f = ⌊(n−1)/3⌋` faulty verifiers. Because `2k > n`, a CONFIRM-quorum and a MISMATCH-quorum can never both form — the outcome is never self-contradictory. |
| 5. Settle | `pouw.dispute` release/slash | Escrow releases the resolution fee only at `release_beat = submit_beat + release_delay`, strictly after the dispute window closes (`release_delay > dispute_window`, enforced in the constructor). |

The deep point: **truth is whatever a deterministic predicate plus a re-executing Byzantine committee agree on, not whatever an oracle reports.** Resolution is reproducible. Any peer — human, agent, regulator, journalist — can pull the evidence set by CID, run the `resolution_spec`, and get the identical outcome digest. There is no privileged endpoint whose word is final, and therefore no endpoint to bribe, subpoena, or capture. PLS minted on a resolved market flows through `Treasury.reward_verified_work`, anti-replayed by proof digest, so the act of *finding truth* is itself the verified-useful-work the economy rewards — not the act of betting on it.

### 11.2 The dispute window: bonded challenge, slash on upheld fault

Re-execution catches honest disagreement; the dispute window catches deliberate fraud, and it directly defends against the **withdraw-before-dispute** attack — a corrupt resolver committing a false outcome and fleeing with the fee before the fraud surfaces. The `DisputeWindowLedger` makes that structurally impossible. Between `submit_beat` and `slashable_until = submit_beat + dispute_window`, any peer may file a bonded `dispute`: they post collateral, name the conflicting outcome, and trigger a fresh committee re-check. The verdict space is exact and asymmetric:

- **DETECTED_FAULT** — a quorum of `MISMATCH` verdicts against a resolver that *claimed success*: the resolver's collateral is **slashed (burned)** and the resolution fee **refunds** to the market escrow. Fraud is never net-profitable.
- **DECLARED_FAULT** — a resolver that *itself* declares it could not resolve (e.g. evidence insufficient): refunded, **no slash**. You are never punished for a fault you owned up to, which removes the perverse incentive to gamble on not being sampled.
- **CONFIRMED** — a quorum of `CONFIRM`: escrow releases, collateral returns.
- **INCONCLUSIVE** — neither quorum forms: settlement *waits* for re-sample or escalation; nothing is paid or slashed.

The challenger's bond is symmetric: a frivolous dispute that the re-check upholds against the challenger forfeits the challenger's bond to the resolver. **Both sides are bonded, so both lying and griefing cost real PLS.** The window length is a market parameter governed by VBANK: high-stakes or ambiguous markets carry longer windows and larger collateral margins (`pouw.collateral.Margin`), sized so the slash always exceeds the payout-at-risk (`payout_at_risk`). This is EigenLayer's "withdrawal delay must exceed the dispute window" invariant, in miniature and enforced at construction time.

### 11.3 Lens as advisory oracle, never as judge

A subtle failure mode of re-execution is the **garbage-in problem**: re-executing a deterministic predicate over a *bad evidence set* faithfully reproduces a bad answer. This is where **LENS** enters — but with a sharply bounded role. Lens is the *claim-reliability layer in design* (lens #5–8): a calibrated-confidence, abstention-capable scorer that produces signed, provenance-cited reliability bundles over evidence records. For a pending resolution, Lens emits an advisory signal: *how reliable is this evidence chain, and should a verifier abstain?*

The non-negotiable architectural rule: **Lens is advisory, never dispositive.** Its output is an *input to verifier judgement and to dispute-worthiness*, not a vote in the quorum. Three properties make this safe:

1. **Abstention over confidence.** Lens is built to *abstain* rather than guess on low-reliability evidence, surfacing INCONCLUSIVE rather than manufacturing a confident wrong answer — the opposite of an LLM oracle that always answers.
2. **Provenance-cited or ignored.** Every Lens reliability claim is gated on attestation plus acyclic provenance (`interpret/distill._gate_relation`); a score whose sources do not resolve on the Web carries zero weight.
3. **Itself re-checkable.** A Lens bundle re-enters PoUW as a SPLIT-verified job, so the *reliability signal itself* is committee-verified work, not a black-box endpoint's say-so.

Lens raises the *probability* that a verifier files a justified dispute against a weak resolution; it never *decides* the resolution. The judge is always the deterministic re-execution plus the BFT quorum. This is the precise inversion of the trusted-LLM-oracle pattern: the model advises the humans-and-agents who verify; it does not replace them.

### 11.4 Personhood: bounding the electorate, not weighting it

The escalation tier of last resort — a VBANK community vote on a genuinely ambiguous claim that the committee cannot resolve — is the most capturable surface in any decentralized market, because token-weighted voting is whale-weighted voting. Markets of Truth defends it with the **personhood gate** (`personhood.gate.require_personhood`): one-person-one-scope via a nullifier, epoch-pinned non-revocation, pairwise DIDs, no PII on-fabric. Escalation votes are **personhood-gated and one-person-one-vote within scope**, not PLS-weighted. An attacker fielding ten thousand agents counts as one person; PLS-stake influences *which markets exist and how disputes are bonded*, never *how many times you vote* in the escalation tier. Agentic-LLM traders pass the *same* gate as humans, so the agent economy cannot become a sybil army at the ballot.

### 11.5 Threat model and mitigations

The system is only credible if it names its attacks. Each row below is a known attack on prediction-market oracles with the specific Pulse mechanism that defends it.

| Attack | How it works | Mitigation in Markets of Truth |
|---|---|---|
| **Bribery / verifier corruption** | Pay verifiers to confirm a false outcome. | A briber must reach `k = ⌊2n/3⌋+1` of an *unpredictable* committee whose membership is unknown at commit time (`select_committee` over a post-commit seed). Corrupting `f = ⌊(n−1)/3⌋` is insufficient by construction; the honest remainder always reaches the true quorum. Briber cost scales with committee size, set by VBANK per market stakes. |
| **Sybil** | Spin up many identities to capture committees, votes, or rewards. | Eligible verifier pool and the escalation electorate are **personhood-gated** (one nullifier per scope); committee draw is over *distinct* gated peers. Sybils cannot multiply jury seats or escalation votes. |
| **Last-mover / withdraw-before-dispute** | Commit a fraudulent outcome and exit with the fee before fraud surfaces. | `release_delay > dispute_window` (constructor-enforced): the fee *cannot* release while a bonded dispute could still land. Detected fault slashes collateral and refunds escrow. |
| **Oracle-capture** | Compromise the single endpoint that reports outcomes. | **There is no oracle to capture.** Resolution is a deterministic `resolution_spec` re-executed by any peer; the "oracle" is a reproducible computation over content-addressed evidence, not an endpoint. |
| **Ambiguous-claim attack** | Author a claim whose resolution is genuinely undecidable, to harvest stakes via a coin-flip resolution. | Committee returns INCONCLUSIVE rather than guessing (quorum's non-quorum path); Lens **abstains** and flags low reliability; unresolvable claims escalate to a personhood-gated VBANK vote with **stake refunded, not split**. VBANK's market-creation policy can require a machine-checkable `resolution_spec` and reject prose claims, pricing ambiguity out at creation. |
| **Grief / frivolous dispute** | Spam disputes to delay settlement and lock escrow. | Challenger posts a symmetric bond; an upheld resolution forfeits the challenger's bond to the resolver. Disputing costs PLS exactly as lying does. |
| **Resolution-spec poisoning** | Smuggle a biased predicate into a popular market. | The `resolution_spec` is a content-addressed, signed, re-executable record; VBANK governs the spec library and the evidence-source allowlist; any peer can audit the predicate by CID before staking. |

### 11.6 Escalation ladder and honest limits

Resolution proceeds along a deliberately **steepening ladder**, cheapest path first: (1) deterministic re-execution; (2) sampled committee re-check to BFT quorum; (3) bonded dispute window with slash-on-fault; (4) on INCONCLUSIVE, a personhood-gated VBANK escalation vote; (5) ultimate fallback — VBANK may *void* a market and refund all stakes. Most markets never leave tier 1–2. Escalation is rare, expensive, and human-judged only when the deterministic and Byzantine layers have genuinely failed to decide.

We are candid about the residual surfaces. The Lens reliability layer is **in design (lens #5–8)**, not yet production-hardened; until it ships, verifier abstention and dispute-filing rest on human and agent judgement over provenance, not on a calibrated score. The personhood verifier remains an injected `PresentationVerifier` adapter (`personhood.verifier`) awaiting its production VC/ZK backend. And the epoch-bound mint seam (R1) must close before resolution rewards are governed by per-epoch demand rather than escrow alone. ### 12.4 Why each new boundary is genuinely separate

A fair challenge to any "needs its own package" claim is over-decomposition. Three tests justify each split here, and every package above passes at least two.

**Distinct trust boundary.** The oracle (#3) decides where real PLS moves; the matching core (#2) decides prices; the personhood proofkit (#12) decides who is a unique person. A bug or a backdoor in any one of these is a fund-loss or manipulation event, so each must be auditable *without* reading the others. The Lens-reliability service (#5) and the agent SDK (#6) are the two boundaries where non-deterministic LLM code lives; quarantining them behind PoUW re-execution and the action-manifest seam means a model swap can never reach consensus state. This is the same architectural conclusion the engine already reached for `interpret` (float-quarantined, SPLIT-verified) and `personhood.verifier` (injected interface).

**Distinct release cadence.** The market-engine knitweb (#1), wallet UX (#8), and MONITOR (#10) ship at consumer-product speed — new market types, UI flows, dashboards — measured in days. The governance package (#13) ships at constitutional speed — measured in ratified votes. The matching core (#2) and oracle (#3) ship at consensus-critical speed — measured in audits. Binding these to one version line would force the slow, audited code to inherit the churn of the fast, cosmetic code, which is precisely the coupling that erodes a substrate's invariants over time.

**Distinct runtime / dependency surface.** The browser transport (#7) runs in untrusted JS/WASM, not stdlib CPython; the agent SDK (#6) and Lens-reliability service (#5) carry LLM-inference dependencies the stdlib-only engine must never import; the indexer (#9) may use a local embedded store the consensus path forbids. Keeping these in separate packages is what lets the engine spine remain pure-Python, stdlib-only, and reproducible — the property that makes a browser peer able to run *byte-for-byte the same* `canonical.encode` and `validate_knit` as a desktop node, with no protocol drift.

## 13. Tokenomics, incentives & the regulatory line

This chapter sets out the economic engine of Markets of Truth — the PLS unit, how it is minted, how curation and forecasting are rewarded, where fees accrue to the vBank treasury, and how personhood holds the whole incentive surface against Sybil capture. It then does the work that no prediction-market plan can responsibly skip: it draws the **regulatory line** in plain terms, and shows why Markets of Truth is a decentralized information-curation and skill-based verified-work venue with an activity-accounting unit — **not** a gambling product and **not** an event-contract securities exchange. We are honest by construction: the claim is backed by what the code mints and refuses to mint, not by a disclaimer. PLS is an activity unit, never a speculative instrument, and every surface in this chapter is designed to keep it that way.

### 13.1 The PLS unit: no premine, PoUW-minted, epoch-bound

PLS ("pulses") is the activity-accounting unit of the Pulse substrate. It meters compute, relay, storage, curation and forecast-verification, and is the integer (wei-style) reward for *verified useful work*. The monetary rules are already encoded in `token/mint.py`, not aspirational. A freshly constructed `Treasury` has minted exactly zero PLS, and there is *intentionally no raw, ungated `mint` method*: the only path to native issuance is `Treasury.reward_verified_work`, which proves the work first as a coinbase `Fiber`, anti-replayed by `job_digest`. Four invariants govern emission, all integer-only and provable:

| Rule | Mechanism (code) | Effect for Markets of Truth |
|---|---|---|
| No premine, no admin mint | `Treasury` starts `total_minted = 0`; no privileged genesis allocation | No founder float to dump; no "house" capitalised ahead of users |
| Demand-gated | Mint fires only on a verified `WorkProof` (`pouw.job.verify`, sampled re-execution) | A bad resolution mints nothing and is slashable; curation that nobody re-verifies pays nothing |
| Bounded by demand | `EmissionPolicy.reward`: `escrow * rate_num // rate_den`, clamped to `min(r, escrow)` | New PLS ≤ what consumers actually spent on verification work |
| Conserved + auditable | Coinbase `Fiber` per mint, cumulative supply in an `Issuance` log | No double-mint; the supply curve is a public, integer audit trail |

The default `EmissionPolicy` is a **1/2 work subsidy** (`rate_num=1, rate_den=2`) with an optional hard `max_supply`. Economically, new money is a matching grant on *proven* verification demand: for every escrow a market-creator or trader burns to have a claim curated and an outcome re-executed, the protocol mints at most that much again to the workers who did and checked that job. There is no inflation knob disconnected from activity — which is precisely the property that distinguishes an activity meter from a wagering chip.

The one **load-bearing gap** we name openly (architecture gap G1): emission is bounded by escrow and `max_supply`, but **not yet by the Pulse epoch**. `core/pulse.py` chains `Beat`/`Pulse` deterministically but is not yet wired to a per-epoch mint window. Roadmap increment **R1 (epoch-bound issuance, PR #196)** closes this: carry an `epoch_mint_cap` on the `Beat`, gate `reward_verified_work` to per-epoch escrowed demand, and settle mints atomically at the epoch boundary. Until R1 lands, PLS is honestly described as *demand-gated but not yet epoch-rate-limited*. This is the single most important tokenomics deliverable before any public exposure, because it turns the heartbeat from a decorative clock into the monetary governor and gives the supply curve a defensible, auditable cadence.

### 13.2 The incentive surface: paying for verified curation, not bets

Markets of Truth has two earning loops, and neither pays for *guessing*. Both pay for *verifiable work that others re-check*.

**(1) Claim curation.** A market begins with a CLAIM — a precisely worded, resolvable proposition with a resolution source and a deadline. Curators stake PLS to assert that a claim is well-formed, non-ambiguous, and resolvable (or to flag that it is not). The **LENS** claim-reliability oracle scores the claim with calibrated confidence and an explicit abstention option (lens #5–8), and every assertion is a provenance-cited relation bundle gated through `interpret.distill._gate_relation` — attestation plus acyclic provenance, or it earns nothing. Curation reward flows only to assertions a committee re-derives.

**(2) Outcome resolution.** When a market closes, resolution is a **PoUW job**, not an operator decision. A worker proposes the outcome by re-executing the resolution procedure against the cited source; a committee selected by `committee.select_committee` (proposer excluded), sized by `sampling.required_samples`, re-executes and emits `Verdict`s; `quorum.tally` aggregates to the BFT `⌊2n/3⌋+1` threshold (`quorum.py`). The result then sits behind a **dispute window** (`DisputeWindowLedger`, with the enforced `release_delay > dispute_window` safety invariant) during which any staker can submit a counter-proof; an upheld dispute slashes the faulty resolver via `dispute_by_quorum` on `DETECTED_FAULT`. Only after the window closes does escrow settle to the honest workers as conservation-preserving `Knit`s, and the bounded reward mint as a coinbase Fiber.

The forecasters' "stake on an outcome" is therefore *not* the thing that pays. What pays is: writing resolvable claims, supplying citable evidence, re-executing resolutions correctly, and successfully disputing wrong ones. A forecaster who merely takes a position and is right captures a *reliability score on their Lens-tracked identity* — a non-transferable, soulbound-style record of calibration — not a cash payout from a pool of losers. This is the structural inversion that keeps the product on the right side of the regulatory line (§13.5): **the economic reward is for verified information work; being directionally correct buys reputation, not a settled wager.**

### 13.3 Fee flows and the vBank treasury

Markets of Truth's revenue line is non-speculative and scales with *verified work volume*, not token price. A protocol fee — a small integer fraction of each settled resolution escrow — is routed as a Knit into a treasury controlled by **VBANK**, the governance knitweb (poll/ranked/liquid voting + tally, end-to-end tested). vBank governs three policy surfaces that an operator would otherwise hold unilaterally, and that centralisation is exactly what we refuse:

| Policy surface | vBank instrument | Why it must be governed, not operated |
|---|---|---|
| Market-creation policy | ranked/liquid vote on claim-class admission | Prevents a "house" from listing manipulable or harmful claim types |
| Fee schedule | poll + tally on `fee_num/fee_den` | No discretionary, off-book rake; fees are public and voted |
| Resolution policy | liquid-democracy vote on resolver standards, dispute parameters | Resolution soundness is constitutional, not a back-office decision |

The treasury funds the personhood-gated reward pool, LENS reliability-scoring compute, MONITOR's public dashboards, and dispute-bounty backstops. Because vBank tallies are deterministic and auditable, and every spend is a Knit, there is no path by which fee income becomes an operator's profit on user losses — the defining economic signature of a gambling house. Value, where it durably accrues, is in **the curated claim graph and the LENS reliability corpus**, not in PLS held for appreciation. PLS meters access to enlarging that graph; the graph is the asset.

### 13.4 Anti-Sybil via personhood

A curation-and-forecasting market collapses the instant one person can be ten thousand identities — a colluding majority would mint the "truth." The defence is the **personhood gate** (maturity 4, ACTIVE), an orthogonal Sybil layer that vBank, the curation reward path, and committee selection all call *before* counting a vote, a stake-weighted assertion, or a reward claim. Its design is privacy-first:

- **One-person-one-scope nullifier** with epoch-pinned non-revocation (`gate.require_personhood`): a human counts once per market scope per epoch, so no actor can field a synthetic majority into a resolution quorum.
- **Pairwise DIDs** and a **revocable proof** model — no PII is ever placed on the fabric; the personhood ticket is *decoupled from the content signature* (the ZK seam), so the credential and the work it authorises cannot be linked to a real-world identity.
- A pluggable `PresentationVerifier` adapter, so the credential backend (a VC/ZK scheme or an aggregator) is injected, not hard-wired. We consume that ecosystem through the seam rather than shipping our own hardware identity.

Crucially, the gate throttles **reward-earning and resolution weight**, not read or play access. Anyone — and any agentic-LLM trader — can browse, learn, and place reputation-only positions; only the *paid* and the *enfranchised in resolution* must prove unique personhood. Agentic LLM traders pass the same gate as humans: an agent's verdict is re-executed and weighted identically, so it has no privileged path to move a resolution. This is the same architectural conclusion the broader market reached — bind agents and votes to a unique human via ZK — expressed as a gate every knitweb calls, not a bolt-on registry.

### 13.5 The regulatory line — honest, specific, and enforced in code

A truth/prediction venue is regulatory-sensitive across three regimes at once: **gambling** (state wagering law), **event-contract derivatives** (CFTC), and **securities**. The 2026 landscape makes the stakes concrete. On 10 June 2026 the CFTC published a proposed rule defining the legality of event contracts and would *ban* contracts "readily susceptible to manipulation" — individual-injury, referee-decision, assassination and conflict-outcome bets — while requiring exchanges to conduct **real-time monitoring** ([ESPN](https://www.espn.com/espn/betting/story/_/id/49019930/cftc-proposes-rules-limiting-prediction-markets-kalshi-sports); [Norton Rose Fulbright](https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/en-us/knowledge/publications/ad8a494a/prediction-markets-at-a-crossroads-preemption-enforcement-and-rulemaking)). The CFTC has brought its first insider-trading action over event contracts, and a federal/state preemption fight is widely expected to reach the Supreme Court ([Stanford Law](https://law.stanford.edu/2026/04/30/prediction-markets-are-surging-heres-what-you-need-to-know/); [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/congress-kalshi-polymarket-prediction-markets-cftc.html)). Over 30 countries already block major prediction platforms ([Chainalysis](https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/crypto-prediction-markets/)). The existing players defend themselves by arguing they are peer-to-peer probability/information venues with no house ([Stanford Report](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2026/04/prediction-markets-facts-gambling-regulation)) — but they still settle cash bets, which keeps them squarely inside the derivatives/gambling debate.

Markets of Truth takes a deliberately different posture. **The core product is decentralized information-curation and skill-based verified work; PLS is activity accounting.** The line is drawn — and enforced — as follows:

- **No fiat cash-out of bets as the core mechanic.** There is no premine, no admin mint, and no operator-run pool that pays winners from losers' fiat. PLS is minted only by `reward_verified_work` against committee-verified jobs; the "earn" can never be separated from re-checked work. A correct forecast yields a **LENS reliability score** (non-transferable reputation), not a settled monetary payout. This removes the constitutive element of a wager — a pooled stake transferred to winners — from the core loop.
- **No house, no operator counterparty, no order-book of cash positions.** Resolution is committee re-execution plus a dispute window, governed by vBank policy. There is no central exchange operating event contracts for its own account, which is the entity the CFTC rule and state gambling law both target.
- **Anti-manipulation is first-class, not a compliance afterthought.** The CFTC's own test — "not readily susceptible to manipulation" with "real-time monitoring" — is met natively: oracle soundness rests on PoUW re-execution, BFT `⌊2n/3⌋+1` quorum, slashing on upheld dispute, personhood-bounded resolution weight, and **MONITOR** publishing locally verifiable market-health, liquidity, agent-activity and dispute dashboards. Manipulable claim *classes* are excludable by vBank market-creation policy before they can ever be listed.
- **KYC / personhood boundary, drawn deliberately.** We require **proof-of-personhood, not identity disclosure**: one-human-one-weight via revocable, pairwise, off-fabric ZK credentials. No PII touches the fabric. This delivers the Sybil/insider-resistance regulators want *without* operating a KYC honeypot or a custodial fiat rail. Should a specific jurisdiction or claim-class require full KYC or fiat settlement, that lives in an isolated, regulated on-ramp adapter at the edge — never inside the trust-minimised `token`/`ledger` core.
- **Jurisdiction strategy.** Default deployment is the information-curation game with reputation-only forecasting and no fiat cash-out — defensible in most jurisdictions as a skill/knowledge product. Any market touching regulated event-outcome classes, or any fiat on-ramp, is gated per-jurisdiction through vBank policy and an external adapter, so the core protocol carries no regulated activity. We avoid the manipulable claim classes the CFTC names outright.

The honest caveats remain on the record: epoch-rate-limiting (R1) is not yet wired, one Erlay anti-amplification interop test is RED (G2), and the LENS float boundary is defended by convention pending R3. We will not expose PLS on any speculative venue, and we will not enable fiat settlement of forecasts as a core mechanic, before R1 closes the activity→money loop and the personhood gate throttles reward and resolution weight in production — because only then is "activity unit, not asset; curation venue, not casino" true in code as well as in prose.

## 14. Technical implementation plan & source structure

This chapter turns the product and architecture theses of the preceding chapters into a concrete engineering blueprint for **Markets of Truth**: the package layout that lands the new market-engine, oracle, matching and agent-SDK code as siblings to the existing Pulse spine; the browser P2P stack that lets a curation market run with no central server; the deterministic matching and resolution path that makes outcomes reproducible by any peer; the testing/CI discipline that protects the sacred invariants; the API seams between Pulse, Lens, Monitor, vBank and the new market packages; and a phased technical sequence that respects what is already built. The guiding principle is **continuity, not rewrite**. The `knitweb/pulse` repository is today ~20.7k LOC of pure-Python, stdlib-first code with 102 property suites and 1183/1184 tests passing; Markets of Truth is an L5 *knitweb* that imports that engine and re-implements nothing — no transport, no gossip, no ledger, no PoUW, no issuance. Everything new is curation/forecasting domain logic standing on a maturity-4/5 substrate.

The discipline matters here more than in any other knitweb: a curation market is regulatory-sensitive, and isolating the resolution/oracle trust boundary into its own audited package is what keeps PLS an activity-accounting unit rather than a speculative instrument bleeding through layer seams.

### 14.2 The browser P2P stack (serverless market runtime)

Markets of Truth must run inside a browser tab so that a player or an agentic trader joins a leaderless market with no origin server in the path. The strategy is **one canonical engine, two runtimes**. The hash-critical, integer-only core (`core.canonical`, `core.crypto`, `core.pulse`, plus `ledger` and the `fabric.web` graph, and crucially the `matching` and `resolution` deciders) is the single source of truth in Python; for the browser it is compiled to WebAssembly so that byte-identical CIDs — and byte-identical market outcomes — are produced on both sides.

The **core** ships first via Pyodide-class CPython-to-WASM ([Pyodide](https://pyodide.org/)) for an exact behavioural match during bootstrap, then is progressively replaced module-by-module by a hand-audited Rust/AssemblyScript reimplementation of `canonical` and `crypto` for size and speed. The discriminating test is differential: the WASM `canonical.encode` must produce the same minimal RFC-8949 §4.2 bytes as the Python one for every fuzz vector, or the build is rejected. Because the canonical encoder is small and hand-rolled (`MAX_DEPTH=64`, sorted-key, no-float, no-indefinite-length), the reimplementation is bounded and auditable.

The **transport** cannot use raw TCP/UDP in a browser, so the `p2p.transport` seam — already abstracted behind `BaseNode._dispatch` and a `Dialer` that routes a `PeerAddress` by its `transport` tag — gains a WebRTC/WebTransport adapter (`transport="webrtc"`). Peers discover one another through a small set of community-run, stateless **signaling relays** (WebRTC data channels over [libp2p-webrtc](https://github.com/libp2p/js-libp2p), plus WebTransport to any node with a public certificate); the relays carry only SDP/ICE handshakes, never content, so they are neither a trust nor an availability bottleneck. Once a reliable, ordered data channel is open, the existing Erlay reconcile (`ReconcileSession`), gossipsub mesh and anti-entropy `sync_from` backstop run *unmodified* over it because they speak only `wire` frames (`read_frame`/`write_frame`), not sockets. A browser tab is therefore a first-class `FabricNode`: it weaves market records, gossips inventory, reconciles deltas, and serves `inv-data` verbatim so a `Claim` or `Position` CID stays byte-identical tab-to-tab.

Persistence uses IndexedDB as the blob store behind the `fabric.items` interface — each browser peer keys received signed frames by CID, exactly mirroring how `FabricNode` stores the verbatim signed frame — and a service worker keeps the node alive across navigations, syncing on reconnect via `sync_from`. The browser-limit defenses already in the engine carry straight over: per-peer byte/probe/recon budgets via `ServeBudget` on the single `_dispatch` seam throttle a flooding peer, the lazy inv→getdata flow means a tab downloads only the market CIDs it lacks, and the `MAX_DEPTH`/`MAX_ITEMS` decoder caps bound post-decode heap explosion (with per-container `MAX_STRING_LEN`/`MAX_ARRAY_LEN` added under R4 before untrusted browser peers widen the surface).

### 14.3 Deterministic matching and resolution

The load-bearing engineering property of a *truth* market is that **matching and resolution are deterministic and reproducible** — two peers given the same hash-pinned market state must derive byte-identical fills and the same resolution, or the market is not trustless, it is an oracle the operator controls. Both deciders are pure, integer-only functions with no float on any canonical path.

**Matching (`truthmarket.matching`).** A market supports two interchangeable mechanisms, both integer-exact. The order-book mode treats each forecast as a signed `truthmarket-order` record; the matcher walks the admitted order set in a total order (`(-price, seq, cid)`), crosses bids and asks, and emits fills as conservation-preserving dual-signed `Knit`s woven into each party's `Braid` — no rounding drift across millions of micro-fills, exactly the property float-denominated EVM settlement cannot structurally guarantee. The scoring-rule mode (an integer LMSR variant) prices a stake against a fixed-point cost function with all division floored and remainders tracked, so the invariant `sum(payouts) == sum(stakes) − fee` holds exactly. Order arrival order is irrelevant: the matcher consumes a *set* of records pinned to a `web_state_cid` and is re-runnable to an identical result, the same order-independence property `vbank.tally` already relies on.

**Resolution (`truthmarket.resolution`).** A market resolves through the same machinery that mints PLS for any useful work — there is no privileged resolver. A resolution claim is registered as a `pouw.job`: a worker proposes an outcome, a committee is selected (`committee.select_committee`, proposer excluded), a sample size `k` is computed (`sampling.required_samples`), each verifier re-executes the outcome derivation and emits a `Verdict`, and `quorum.tally` aggregates to a Byzantine `⌊2n/3⌋+1` outcome. Because resolution must tolerate good-faith disagreement and active manipulation, it is registered **SPLIT-verified**, so settlement additionally requires a *closed dispute window with no upheld dispute* (`pouw.dispute.DisputeWindowLedger`, with the enforced `release_delay > dispute_window` safety invariant). Only then does escrow release and the market pay out. The **LENS oracle** (`lens.oracle`) feeds this path as a calibrated, abstaining signal — not a decider: it scores the resolution claim's reliability from attested, acyclic provenance (`distill._gate_relation`-style membership + acyclicity + attestation gates) and returns calibrated confidence with an explicit *abstain* when sources are insufficient. The oracle never *asserts* an outcome; it ranks and flags, exactly as Lens backends rank candidates but the Web's attestation graph remains the sole arbiter of truth. This is the architectural answer to oracle-soundness and anti-manipulation: a manipulator cannot publish a payout that committee re-execution will not reproduce, and a sybil cannot inflate verifier weight past the `personhood.gate.require_personhood` one-person-one-scope nullifier.

### 14.4 Testing and CI

The test pyramid mirrors the existing markers: `property` (deterministic, fast, the bulk — fuzzing `canonical`, crypto parity, ledger conservation, **matching idempotence and resolution determinism**), `interop` (two-or-more-node networked convergence — Erlay byte-budget, mesh propagation, identical `web_state_root` across peers after a market resolves), and `knitweb` (domain end-to-end — a full stake→curate→forecast→resolve cycle paying out PLS). New work obeys four hard merge gates:

- **Differential WASM gate.** Every `canonical`/`crypto` fuzz vector must yield byte-identical output in Python and WASM — the load-bearing test for the browser runtime.
- **Determinism gate (matching + resolution).** The same market state must produce identical fills and identical resolution CIDs under shuffled record arrival order and across Python/WASM. A market whose outcome depends on order is a release-blocker.
- **No-float-near-canonical gate.** A static check plus a runtime property test asserts no float reaches `canonical.encode`, hardening Gap G3 from convention into an enforced type boundary at the interpret→fabric and matching seams.
- **Import-contract gate.** An import-linter layered contract enforces §14.1's dependency direction; a `truthmarket→vbank-internals` import fails CI.

CI runs on GitHub Actions across CPython 3.12 and a 3.14 free-threaded lane (the core is I/O-bound and runs unchanged), executes the full property suite on every push, and gates interop tests on a label to keep PRs fast. The currently-RED `test_p2p_erlay_reconcile.py::test_live_byte_budget_throttles_a_hammering_peer` is a release-blocker — no reconcile/mesh change lands until it is green — and per cross-session coordination it is reviewed in the p2p lane, not raced.

### 14.5 API seams between components

The components integrate through narrow, signed, content-addressed contracts rather than shared mutable state:

| Producer → Consumer | Seam | Contract |
|---|---|---|
| Pulse → token / vBank | `Beat.epoch_mint_cap` | per-epoch supply ceiling gates `Treasury.reward_verified_work` |
| truthmarket → PoUW | `pouw.job` (SPLIT) | resolution as verifiable, dispute-windowed work earning PLS |
| Lens oracle → resolution | `OracleReport` | calibrated confidence + abstention + provenance CIDs; advisory, never deciding |
| personhood → truthmarket / vBank | `require_personhood` | one-person-one-scope Sybil gate before order/curation/vote |
| agent_sdk → truthmarket | `GET /heartbeat?sid=` | stateless action-manifest of every currently-legal move |
| fabric/pouw → Monitor | read-only metrics | `web_state_root`, liquidity, open disputes, agent activity; no write path |
| seed → fabric | root CID | self-verifying source bundle the dapp ships over its own Web |

Monitor is deliberately **read-only**: it subscribes to `p2p.metrics`, `fabric` state roots and `pouw` settlement counters and never holds a write capability, so the market-health and dispute dashboards can never become an attack surface — the same `127.0.0.1`, stdlib-only, gracefully-degrading discipline the existing `knitweb_monitor` already enforces.

### 14.6 Phased technical sequence

**Phase A — close the loop.** Ship `pulse.epoch` binding issuance to the heartbeat (Gap G1), fix the RED Erlay byte-budget test (G2), and turn the no-float rule into a type boundary (G3). All solo-lane, no p2p races. **Phase B — browser core.** Pyodide-WASM core + WebRTC transport adapter, differential gate green, a two-tab interop convergence demo. **Phase C — market engine.** Promote `knitwebs/truthmarket` from stub to a live knitweb: `Claim`/`Market`/`Position` records, the integer matcher, and SPLIT-verified resolution wired to `pouw.dispute`. **Phase D — oracle + agents.** Land `lens.oracle` (calibrated confidence, abstention) and the `agent_sdk` so agentic-LLM traders curate and forecast as first-class peers under the personhood gate. **Phase E — govern + harden.** `vbank.marketpolicy` governs fees/market-creation/resolution policy; per-container canonical limits, EC-point validation and the equivocation witness ship; the audited Rust/AssemblyScript core replaces Pyodide. Each phase ends with a `seed`-published, CID-pinned release, so progress is itself woven into the Web it builds — the market, its source and its economy all living on one content-addressed substrate with no privileged host.



## 15. Roadmap, KPIs & Risks to Top-10

The preceding chapters argued that **Markets of Truth** is a defensible, genuinely novel category: a serverless, P2P, agentic information-curation market where verified humans and autonomous LLM traders stake PLS to curate CLAIMS and forecast OUTCOMES, and where resolution is settled by PoUW re-execution, a dispute window, Lens reliability scoring, and a personhood anti-sybil gate. This chapter converts that argument into an executable plan. It sequences the work into four phases (**P0 Foundation → P1 Serverless Market MVP → P2 Agentic + Economy → P3 Scale & Top-10**), each with entry/exit criteria, owning components, and KPIs; it names the single critical-path chain that, if it slips, slips everything; and it then confronts the real risks — **regulatory first**, because a truth/prediction market is the most regulatory-sensitive product on the substrate — followed by oracle capture, P2P matching maturity, cold-start liquidity, manipulation, and LLM hallucination. It closes with the affirmative case for why this is a credible, unique top-10 dapp.

### 15.1 Phased roadmap

The phases are deliberately back-loaded on novelty. P0 and P1 are mostly *closing* and *wiring* work on subsystems already at maturity 4–5 in the architecture state-of-the-union (full ledger, PoUW verify/quorum/committee/dispute/collateral, fabric Web, p2p reconcile/mesh, vBank, personhood gate). That de-risks the genuinely new P2/P3 work — the agentic trader loop and the PLS economy — by giving it a hardened substrate to stand on.

#### P0 — Foundation (Q3 2026)

P0 finishes the load-bearing seams. Nothing here is user-visible, but every later KPI depends on it.

- **P0-M1 — Epoch-bound issuance (R1).** Bind `EmissionPolicy` (`token/mint.py`) to the `Beat`/`Pulse` heartbeat (`core/pulse.py`): carry an `epoch_mint_cap` on the Beat, gate `Treasury.reward_verified_work` to per-epoch escrowed demand, settle mints atomically at the epoch boundary. **Owner: PULSE + token.** This is the single highest-leverage increment — it turns the heartbeat from a decorative clock into the monetary governor and is the precondition for any honest claim that PLS is activity-accounting, not speculation.
- **P0-M2 — `MarketKnitweb` resolution primitive.** A new L5 knitweb modelling a CLAIM as a content-addressed fabric record, an OUTCOME forecast as an escrowed position, and *resolution* as a `SynapticCompileJob`-style PoUW job: re-executable resolution criteria, committee re-execution (`pouw/quorum.tally`, `⌊2n/3⌋+1`), and a `DisputeWindowLedger` window with the enforced `release_delay > dispute_window` invariant. **Owner: VBANK + PULSE pouw.**

Float-to-type-boundary (R3) lands at the LENS scoring seam so a calibrated confidence can never leak a float onto a canonical path. **Owner: PULSE core/p2p + LENS.**
- **P0-M4 — CID-stability sign-off.** The hard cross-session gate: the `MarketKnitweb` schema may not land until PULSE signs off that record CIDs are byte-stable across the relay hop. **Owner: PULSE, blocking the market track.**

*Exit KPIs:* test suite green (the RED Erlay test is the gate); epoch mint reconciles to escrowed demand within ±0 PLS over a 100-epoch simulation; a single claim resolves deterministically under committee re-execution in a local harness.

#### P1 — Serverless Market MVP (Q4 2026–Q1 2027)

P1 lands the defining property: a market that is *created, traded, and resolved* with no authoritative server, the application shipping its own client over the same content-addressed Web (`core.canonical.cid`, fabric inv→getdata→inv-data flow) that carries the markets.

- **P1-M1 — Create→stake→resolve loop.** Market creation gated by vBank policy; positions are escrowed Knits (`ledger.knit`, integer-exact, conservation-preserving); resolution settles via PoUW re-execution + dispute window. **Owner: VBANK + MOLGANG-equivalent market client.**

*Exit KPIs:* the MVP runs with **zero central server** (verified by killing any relay and confirming a fresh peer can bootstrap, fetch client, stake, and see a resolution); ≥10 external peers; ≥50 markets created and resolved; convergent `web_state_root`; first non-team participant earns PLS for a correct verified curation.

#### P2 — Agentic + Economy (Q2 2027)

P2 lands the differentiator: the LENS reliability oracle and agentic LLM traders, plus a real fee/governance economy.

- **P2-M1 — LENS reliability oracle.** Calibrated-confidence + abstention scoring (lens #5–8): for each claim, deterministic retrieve + gated distill emits a *signed, provenance-cited* reliability bundle that itself re-enters PoUW as a SPLIT-verified job. The oracle abstains rather than guesses on low-evidence claims. **Owner: LENS.**
- **P2-M2 — Agentic trader loop.** LLM traders curate, forecast, and dispute as first-class signed peers passing the same personhood gate and the same committee verification as humans. **Owner: LENS + PULSE personhood.**
- **P2-M3 — vBank fee/resolution governance.** vBank poll/ranked/liquid voting governs market-creation fees, the `epoch_mint_cap`, and resolution policy (criteria templates, dispute parameters). **Owner: VBANK.**

*Exit KPIs:* ≥30% of curation/resolution volume produced or verified by agentic traders; ≥100 DAU; D7 retention ≥20%; first vBank proposal that *changes* a fee or resolution parameter passes and takes effect at the next epoch; zero successful Sybil mint (audited); Lens calibration error (Brier/ECE) within target on a held-out claim set.

#### P3 — Scale & Top-10 (H2 2027–Q1 2028)

- **P3-M1 — Mesh scale.** Erlay reconcile + anti-entropy validated at 1k+ concurrent peers under adversarial frames.
- **P3-M2 — Equivocation witness (R5).** Structural `fork_height`/`equivocation_witness` on `Fiber`, gossiped fork proofs.
- **P3-M3 — Growth push.** Public listing, tournaments with PLS prize pools funded via crowdfunding knitweb, partner curation cohorts.

*Exit KPIs:* 1,000+ concurrent peers; 1,000+ weekly active verified participants; agentic share ≥40%; D30 retention ≥10%; top-10 placement in the target category.

The KPI dashboard targets the metrics a serious information-curation market lives or dies on — not raw wallet counts, which are trivially Sybil-inflated, but **resolution accuracy** and **verified-human activity**, which the personhood gate lets Markets of Truth report when most competitors structurally cannot.

### 15.3 Risks and mitigations

#### Regulatory exposure (first, and largest)

**The risk.** A truth/prediction market is the most regulatory-sensitive product imaginable on this substrate. In the US, the CFTC published a [proposed rule for prediction markets on June 10, 2026](https://bitcoinfoundation.org/news/regulation/us-prediction-markets-reg/) and has moved to bar certain sports event contracts (officiating, player-injury outcomes), per [ESPN](https://www.espn.com/espn/betting/story/_/id/49019930/cftc-proposes-rules-limiting-prediction-markets-kalshi-sports); combined Kalshi/Polymarket volume quadrupled from under $5B (Sep 2025) to $24B (Apr 2026), drawing [Congressional scrutiny over insider trading and gambling](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/congress-kalshi-polymarket-prediction-markets-cftc.html), while states including Wisconsin, New York and Illinois insist these are gambling under [state regulation](https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/en-us/knowledge/publications/ad8a494a/prediction-markets-at-a-crossroads-preemption-enforcement-and-rulemaking). A serverless, self-distributing dapp has no obvious legal entity to absorb that exposure.

**The mitigation — and the honest framing.** Markets of Truth is designed, from the ground up, **not as a gambling product** but as a decentralized **information-curation and skill-based verified-work** protocol with **activity-accounting PLS**. The distinctions are structural, not cosmetic: (1) PLS is minted *only* as demand-gated reward for committee-verified useful curation (`token/mint.py` has no premine and no admin mint), so it is utility-shaped, not investment-shaped — never a speculative instrument and never wagered against the house; (2) the product's value is the *verified information graph*, not a payout on a binary event — the strongest available posture against both securities and gambling characterizations; (3) personhood is privacy-first (revocable proofs, pairwise DIDs, nullifiers, **no PII on-fabric**), compliant-by-construction against GDPR-class regimes; (4) market-creation policy, resolution policy, and any event-contract-like surface sit *behind explicit vBank governance gates*, so jurisdiction-sensitive market types can be excluded by policy. Stated plainly: "the protocol has no central operator" is a technical fact, not a legal shield. Launch sequencing must favour jurisdictions with clear digital-asset frameworks, hard-exclude regulated categories (sports outcomes, securities-priced events) by default, and obtain qualified counsel before PLS has any secondary-market liquidity. This is a non-negotiable gate, not an afterthought.

#### Oracle capture

**The risk.** Resolution is where every prediction market is attacked. UMA's token-weighted optimistic oracle has been [publicly compromised](https://thedefiant.io/news/markets/usd85m-polymarket-dispute-over-strategy-s-may-bitcoin-sale-puts-uma-s-token-voting-oracle-on): in March 2025 a single actor controlling 25% of voting power falsely settled a $7M contract, and a 2026 investigation found that in most disputed markets over half the votes came from the ten largest wallets, with [~20% of disputes including a voter with a financial stake in the outcome](https://orochi.network/blog/oracle-manipulation-in-polymarket-2025). Token-weighted voting *is* the capture surface.

**The mitigation.** Markets of Truth does not resolve by token-weighted vote. Resolution is **PoUW re-execution**: a committee selected by `committee.select_committee` (proposer excluded), sized by `sampling.required_samples`, re-runs the claim's deterministic resolution criteria byte-for-byte and aggregates to a BFT `⌊2n/3⌋+1` quorum (`pouw/quorum.tally`), backed by a dispute window with **slash-on-upheld-fault** collateral (`pouw/dispute.py`). Capture requires controlling two-thirds of a *randomly sampled, personhood-gated* committee and surviving a dispute window — categorically harder than buying a token majority. The LENS reliability oracle adds a second, provenance-cited signal that abstains on weak evidence rather than forcing a verdict.

#### P2P matching maturity

**The risk.** A market needs traders matched, and browser-to-browser P2P remains the weakest link: WebRTC is "riddled with challenges related to routers, NAT layers, VPNs, and firewalls" and falls back on signaling/STUN/TURN that quietly re-centralizes the system. Thin browser peers also cannot shoulder full reconcile/verify work.

**The mitigation.** We do not require browser peers to be validators. The carrier seam (`p2p/base_node.py`) separates transport from role, enabling a **tiered topology**: durable native "spine" nodes carry reconcile, mesh relay, and committee verification; browser participants run a thin transport and act as light clients that author positions, fetch via the lazy inv flow, and *cryptographically verify CIDs locally* without trusting the server that relayed them. Matching is not a central order book but escrow against a market record on the Web. Signaling is treated as a commodity relay (multiple swappable endpoints, bootstrap addresses gossiped). Honest caveat: until decentralized WebRTC signaling is universally robust, a small set of always-on spine nodes is load-bearing; we own and budget for that.

#### Cold-start liquidity

**The risk.** [Cold-start liquidity is the primary reason prediction markets fail within 90 days](https://www.weex.com/wiki/article/top-prediction-market-platforms-in-2026-what-users-compare-g6qve5bfww9a3i8n4pfftidv): a market with no counterparties is indistinguishable from a dead one, and a thin committee cannot be safely sampled for a BFT quorum.

**The mitigation.** Three levers. First, **agentic traders as bootstrap liquidity**: LENS-driven traders generate genuine, verifiable curation and forecasts from the first session, seeding both market depth and the mint loop without a human crowd. Second, **epoch-gated emission as a cold-start governor** (R1): a per-epoch mint cap keeps early rewards generous-but-bounded so emission cannot be drained before the market thickens. Third, a *bounded-loss* liquidity primitive for thin markets — an [LMSR/LS-LMSR-style scoring rule keeps spreads tight at bootstrap with bounded maker loss](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.11990) — implemented integer-only so it never touches a float near a canonical path. Caveat: agentic liquidity must remain *demand-gated*, never free minting.

#### Manipulation

**The risk.** Beyond oracle capture: wash-curation to farm rewards, collusive committees rubber-stamping a confederate, insider trading on claims whose outcome an actor privately controls (a live concern even on [regulated venues](https://www.venable.com/insights/publications/2026/04/kalshi-and-polymarket-move-to-curb-insider)), and griefing via spam transfers.

**The mitigation.** PoUW is defended in depth: worker-excluded committees, computed sample sizes, BFT aggregation, and **staked collateral with slash-on-`DETECTED_FAULT`** make collusion negative-EV. The personhood gate (one-person-one-scope nullifiers) caps how many identities one actor can field. vBank governance can quarantine or sanction claims and curators. R4 (EC-point validation, per-container budgets) and receiver-side flow control (G6) close spam vectors. Insider-controlled outcomes are mitigated by policy: resolution criteria must be *publicly re-executable*, which structurally excludes private-information event contracts.

#### LLM hallucination into canonical state

**The risk.** Agentic traders are LLMs, and in a shared content-addressed graph "a single hallucinated entry can spread to every downstream agent that queries it." A plausible-but-false claim, once canonicalized, is permanently addressable and can poison every later distill and cited Lens answer.

**The mitigation.** No LLM writes directly to canonical state. Distillation is **gated and verified, never trusted**: `interpret/distill._gate_relation` requires attestation plus acyclic provenance, and the resulting manifest re-enters as a SPLIT-verified PoUW job demanding deterministic re-check *and* a closed dispute window *and* no upheld dispute. The LENS reliability layer *abstains* on low-evidence claims rather than fabricating confidence, and every score is provenance-cited back to grounding CIDs. The candid limit: re-execution proves *reproducibility*, not *truth* — subtly-wrong-but-reproducible claims ultimately lean on the human-plus-committee curation economy, which is exactly why the dispute/slash incentives and personhood gate matter so much here.

### 15.4 Conclusion — a credible, unique top-10 dapp

Strip away the hype around "AI x prediction markets" and ask what would actually be *new*. Markets of Truth is unique on a conjunction of axes no incumbent holds at once. Its resolution does not rest on a captured token-weighted oracle but on **PoUW re-execution with a slashable dispute window** — a direct answer to the single hardest failure mode of every prediction market shipping today. Its currency is **earned, not printed**: PLS exists only as the epoch-governed, demand-gated reward for curation a BFT committee re-executed and a dispute window cleared — activity-accounting, never a wager or a speculative instrument. Its **agents are first-class but never trusted**: LLM traders curate, forecast, and dispute, yet nothing they assert touches canonical state until it survives gated distillation, deterministic re-verification, personhood-gated curation, and economically-staked dispute. And it is **fully serverless and self-distributing**, shipping its own client over the same Web that carries its markets, with no app store and no kill switch. Crucially, it is framed honestly and built honestly as **decentralized information-curation and verified work**, not gambling — with regulatory exclusions enforced by governance, not hoped for. The hard problems are real and we have named the regulatory one first. The opportunity is that a venue where verified humans and reasoning machines jointly curate *provably-resolved truth*, and are paid only for usefulness, is precisely the empty intersection the agent economy has been missing — and being its reference implementation is a credible, defensible path into the top-10 of a category that does not yet have one.
